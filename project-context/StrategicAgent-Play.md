# Grant Proposal Outline

1\. Cover Page

* Project Title  
* Applicant Organization(s)  
* Contact Information  
* Date  
* Funding Program Name

---

2\. Executive Summary

* Brief overview of the project, objectives, and funding request.  
* Why this project matters for Baie Verte and the funder’s priorities.

---

3\. Project Description

* Background/Need:  
* Context (e.g., Firefly Metals project, local economic opportunity, workforce/supplier gaps).  
* Evidence of need (data, community input, alignment with funder’s goals).  
* Objectives:  
* Clear, measurable goals (e.g., “Train 50 local residents for mining jobs by 2025”).  
* Activities/Work Plan:  
* What you will do, when, and how (phases, milestones, partners).  
* Expected Outcomes:  
* Tangible results (jobs created, businesses supported, infrastructure improved).

---

4\. Project Team & Partners

* Key personnel, roles, and qualifications.  
* Partnerships (e.g., Firefly Metals, Town Council, colleges, Indigenous groups).

---

5\. Budget & Funding Request

* Total project cost, amount requested, and other sources of funding (cash/in-kind).  
* Budget breakdown (personnel, equipment, training, outreach, etc.).

---

6\. Evaluation & Reporting

* How you will measure success (KPIs, reporting schedule).  
* Commitment to transparency and accountability.

---

7\. Sustainability & Impact

* How the project will have lasting benefits beyond the funding period.  
* Plans for ongoing support, scaling, or replication.

---

8\. Appendices (as required)

* Letters of support/partnership  
* Maps, data tables, resumes, organizational documents

---

# Grant Writing Tips

* Align with Funder Priorities: Use language from the funder’s guidelines and show how your project advances their goals.  
* Be Clear and Concise: Avoid jargon; use bullet points and short paragraphs.  
* Use Data and Evidence: Support your need statement and expected outcomes with local data and research.  
* Show Community Support: Include letters of support and evidence of engagement.  
* Demonstrate Capacity: Highlight your team’s experience and partnerships.  
* Provide a Realistic Budget: Ensure costs are justified and match the work plan.  
* Plan for Evaluation: Clearly state how you’ll track and report on progress.  
* Proofread: Double-check for errors and ensure all required documents are included.
