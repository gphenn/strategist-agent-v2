## Document Purpose

This document establishes the project management framework for the Baie Verte Economic Development Project. The framework defines project management processes, methodologies, tools, and standards to ensure consistent, effective project delivery across all phases.

**Objectives:**
- Establish standardized project management processes
- Define roles, responsibilities, and accountabilities
- Provide tools and templates for project management
- Ensure consistent project delivery across all work packages
- Support effective decision-making and risk management
- Enable project monitoring, control, and reporting

---

## Project Management Approach

### Methodology

**Hybrid Approach:** Combining predictive and adaptive project management methodologies

- **Predictive Elements:** 
  - Detailed planning for Phase 1 (Foundation & Assessment)
  - Structured work packages with defined deliverables
  - Clear timelines and milestones
  - Formal governance and decision-making

- **Adaptive Elements:**
  - Iterative assessment and refinement
  - Flexible response to new information
  - Continuous stakeholder engagement
  - Regular review and adjustment

### Core Principles

1. **Stakeholder-Centric:** All decisions consider stakeholder needs and impacts
2. **Data-Driven:** Decisions based on comprehensive assessments and analysis
3. **Transparent:** Open communication and clear decision-making processes
4. **Collaborative:** Cross-functional working groups and stakeholder engagement
5. **Quality-Focused:** High standards for deliverables and processes
6. **Risk-Aware:** Proactive risk identification and mitigation
7. **Outcome-Oriented:** Focus on achieving desired outcomes and success criteria

## Project Structure & Governance

### Governance Structure

**Steering Committee:**
- **Role:** Strategic oversight, major decisions, resource approval
- **Composition:** Municipal representative, Firefly Metals, provincial government, community representative, technical advisor
- **Frequency:** Bi-weekly meetings
- **Authority:** Strategic decisions, budget approval, major scope changes

**Project Management Team:**
- **Project Manager:** Overall coordination and delivery
- **Technical Lead:** Technical assessments and analysis
- **Stakeholder Coordinator:** Engagement and communication
- **Financial Analyst:** Financial assessments and modeling
- **Communications Specialist:** Communication and reporting

**Working Groups:**
- **Infrastructure Working Group:** Infrastructure assessments and planning
- **Business Development Working Group:** Business opportunity analysis
- **Workforce Development Working Group:** Workforce assessments and planning
- **Community Services Working Group:** Community services assessments

### Decision-Making Framework

**Decision Authority Levels:**

1. **Steering Committee:**
   - Strategic decisions
   - Budget approval (>$10,000)
   - Major scope changes
   - Resource allocation
   - Risk mitigation strategies

2. **Project Manager:**
   - Day-to-day operational decisions
   - Budget approval (<$10,000)
   - Resource allocation within approved budget
   - Issue resolution
   - Process improvements

3. **Working Group Leads:**
   - Technical decisions within working group scope
   - Work package execution decisions
   - Quality assurance within work packages

**Decision Process:**
1. Issue/opportunity identified
2. Options analyzed
3. Recommendation developed
4. Decision brief prepared
5. Appropriate authority reviews
6. Decision made and documented
7. Decision communicated
8. Implementation monitored

## Work Package Management

### Work Package Structure

**Standard Work Package Components:**
1. **Objective:** Clear statement of work package purpose
2. **Activities:** Detailed list of activities
3. **Deliverables:** Specific, measurable deliverables
4. **Timeline:** Start date, end date, milestones
5. **Resources:** Required personnel, budget, tools
6. **Dependencies:** Prerequisites and dependencies
7. **Success Criteria:** Measurable success criteria
8. **Risks:** Identified risks and mitigation

### Work Package Execution Process

1. **Initiation:**
   - Work package kickoff meeting
   - Team assignment
   - Resource allocation
   - Timeline confirmation

2. **Planning:**
   - Detailed activity planning
   - Resource planning
   - Risk identification
   - Quality planning

3. **Execution:**
   - Activity execution
   - Regular status updates
   - Issue management
   - Quality assurance

4. **Monitoring:**
   - Progress tracking
   - Budget monitoring
   - Risk monitoring
   - Quality monitoring

5. **Closure:**
   - Deliverable completion
   - Quality review
   - Lessons learned
   - Handoff to next phase

### Work Package Status Reporting

**Status Categories:**
- **On Track:** Progressing as planned
- **At Risk:** Potential delays or issues identified

- **Complete:** Work package completed

**Status Report Elements:**
- Progress summary
- Accomplishments

### Risk Escalation

**Escalation Levels:**
- **Low Risk:** Documented, reviewed monthly
