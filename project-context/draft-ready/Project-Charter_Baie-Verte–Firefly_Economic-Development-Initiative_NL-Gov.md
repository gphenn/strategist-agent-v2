# Project Charter: Baie Verte–Firefly Economic Development Initiative

## 1\. Project Title

Baie Verte–Firefly Economic Development Initiative

## 2\. Project Purpose & Vision

To leverage the Firefly Metals mining project as a driver for sustainable economic diversification, job creation, and community well-being in Baie Verte, aligning with the Province of Newfoundland and Labrador’s strategic priorities for regional development and responsible resource management.

## 3\. Objectives

* Create high-quality, sustainable employment opportunities for residents of Baie Verte and surrounding communities.  
* Foster the growth of local businesses and suppliers, strengthening the regional economy.  
* Upgrade critical infrastructure (housing, transportation, digital connectivity) to support population growth and business activity.  
* Maximize the impact of provincial and federal funding programs through coordinated applications and project management.  
* Ensure environmental stewardship, Indigenous partnership, and robust community engagement throughout the project lifecycle.

## 4\. Scope

In Scope:

* Workforce training and upskilling programs in partnership with provincial agencies and educational institutions.  
* Supplier readiness and business development initiatives for local SMEs.  
* Infrastructure planning and project execution (housing, transport, utilities, digital) in collaboration with municipal and provincial authorities.  
* Grant writing and funding application management for all eligible programs.  
* Community and Indigenous engagement activities, including public forums and partnership agreements.

Out of Scope:

* Direct management of Firefly Metals’ mining operations.  
* Activities outside the Baie Verte region.

## 5\. Key Stakeholders

* Project Sponsor: Provincial Government of Newfoundland and Labrador (Department of Industry, Energy and Technology)  
* Strategic Partners: Baie Verte Town Council, Firefly Metals, ACOA, CBDC  
* Community Partners: Local businesses, Indigenous communities, residents, educational institutions

## 6\. Governance Structure

* Task Force: Multi-stakeholder steering committee (Province, Town, Firefly, ACOA/CBDC, Indigenous, business reps)  
* Project Manager: \[Name/To be assigned, Provincial or joint\]  
* Working Groups: Workforce, Supplier, Infrastructure, Engagement, Funding

## 7\. Major Deliverables

* Regional workforce and supplier audit reports  
* Training and supplier development programs with provincial support  
* Infrastructure project proposals and funding applications for provincial/federal programs  
* Community engagement plan and public forum schedule  
* Quarterly progress and KPI reports for provincial oversight

## 8\. Timeline

* Mobilization: 0–3 months  
* Program Design & Funding: 3–6 months  
* Execution: 6–18 months  
* Monitoring & Adaptation: Ongoing

## 9\. Success Criteria / KPIs

* Number of new jobs created and training completions  
* $ value of local procurement and new business creation  
* Amount of provincial/federal funding secured  
* Infrastructure projects completed on time/budget  
* Community and Indigenous satisfaction metrics

## 10\. Risks & Mitigation

* Workforce shortages: Early training, targeted recruitment, provincial support  
* Supplier gaps: Supplier readiness program, business support, provincial procurement engagement  
* Funding delays: Dedicated grant-writing team, early applications, provincial advocacy  
* Community concerns: Transparent engagement, regular forums, Indigenous partnership
