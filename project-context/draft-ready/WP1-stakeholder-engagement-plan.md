# Stakeholder Engagement Plan

## Baie Verte Economic Development Project

**Project/Initiative:** Baie Verte Economic Development Plan for Firefly Metals Mining Expansion  
**Analysis Owner:** Project Management Team  
**Date Created:** January 2025  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Analysis Scope:** All stakeholders involved in or affected by the economic development project  
**Review Period:** Monthly during Phase 1, quarterly during implementation

**Objective:** Establish comprehensive stakeholder engagement strategy to ensure successful project delivery, maintain positive relationships, and secure necessary support and resources for economic development initiatives.  
**Success Criteria:** 
- All key stakeholders identified and mapped
- Engagement strategies developed for each stakeholder group
- Communication protocols established and operational
- Stakeholder support maintained or increased throughout project
- No stakeholder-related project delays or conflicts

**Time Horizon:** Project duration (Phase 1: 6 months, Full project: 5+ years)

## Power-Interest Matrix

### Quadrant Classification

**High Power, High Interest (Manage Closely)**

- **Town of Baie Verte Municipal Government (Mayor, Council, CAO):** Primary decision-makers with formal authority over municipal resources and approvals. Critical for project success.
- **Firefly Metals Management (President & CEO, VP Operations):** Key business partner with direct influence on service contracts and project requirements. Essential for achieving 80-85% service capture target.
- **Qalipu First Nation:** Required consultation partner with regulatory influence. Critical for environmental/resource impact approvals.
- **Baie Verte Peninsula Mining Innovation Task Force (Chair: Shannon Lewis):** Implementation coordination body with community influence and project management role.

**High Power, Low Interest (Keep Satisfied)**

- **Provincial Government Agencies:** Control funding and regulatory approvals but may have competing priorities. Need to keep informed and satisfied with project progress.
- **Federal Government Agencies:** Control federal funding programs but may have limited direct involvement. Need periodic updates and alignment with federal priorities.

**Low Power, High Interest (Keep Informed)**

- **BVP Chamber of Commerce:** Represents local business interests, high engagement needed for business development initiatives.
- **Local Service Businesses:** Direct beneficiaries of service opportunities, need regular communication about opportunities and requirements.
- **Local Workforce:** Directly affected by employment opportunities, need clear communication about training and job opportunities.

**Low Power, Low Interest (Monitor)**

- **Neighboring Communities:** May be indirectly affected, monitor for potential concerns or opportunities for regional collaboration.

## Risk Assessment

### Stakeholder Risks

**High Risk Stakeholders:**

- **Qalipu First Nation:** Risk of consultation delays or opposition if environmental concerns not adequately addressed. Mitigation: Early and ongoing consultation, environmental protection measures.
- **Provincial/Federal Government:** Risk of funding delays or denials if project not aligned with government priorities. Mitigation: Alignment with government priorities, strong project proposals, relationship building.

**Risk Scenarios:**

- **Scenario 1:** Key supporter (Municipal Government) withdraws support due to funding concerns
  - **Response:** Strengthen funding strategy, demonstrate project value, address concerns
- **Scenario 2:** Opposition builds from Qalipu First Nation due to environmental concerns
  - **Response:** Enhanced environmental protection measures, increased consultation, benefit sharing
- **Scenario 3:** Firefly Metals reduces local content commitment
  - **Response:** Demonstrate local capacity, strengthen partnership, highlight benefits
