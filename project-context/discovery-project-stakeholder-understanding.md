# Strategic Discovery: Baie Verte Economic Development Project

## Project Readiness Assessment - Dimension 3: Stakeholder Understanding

**Date:** January 2025  
**Strategic Context:** Regional Economic Development + Mining Services  
**Active Modules:** Regional Economic Development Strategic Module, Mining Services Economic Development Module

---

## Executive Summary

The Baie Verte Economic Development Project involves a complex stakeholder landscape spanning local government, regional communities, provincial and federal agencies, business entities, and indigenous organizations. The project benefits from strong existing relationships with Firefly Metals and government agencies, while navigating regional independence dynamics and indigenous consultation requirements.

**Stakeholder Complexity:** High - Multi-level government involvement, business relationships, regional coordination challenges, and indigenous consultation requirements.

---

## 1. Local Governance Structure

### Town of Baie Verte Municipal Government
**Structure:**
- **Elected Officials:** Mayor and Council
- **Administrative Staff:** Chief Administrative Officer, Clerk
- **Authority:** Municipal governance and municipal infrastructure decisions

**Key Characteristics:**
- Largest town on Baie Verte Peninsula (BVP)
- Important distinction: Town of Baie Verte vs. Baie Verte Peninsula
- 21 communities on BVP (some Municipalities, others Local Service Districts)

### Economic Development Task Force
**Organization:** Baie Verte Peninsula Mining Innovation Task Force
**Established:** 2023 in response to regional economic downturn from mine closures
**Chair:** Shannon Lewis
**Administration:** Regional Economic Development Consultant Contract administered by BVP Chamber of Commerce

**Scope & Purpose:**
- Represents community, business, industry, government, education, and others
- Provides platform for strategic approach across public and private stakeholders
- Maximizes socio-economic development and capacity building
- Aligns with needs of represented groups

**Strategic Context:**
- Created due to absence of strong regional economic development presence
- Fills critical coordination gap in the region

### Decision-Making Authority
**Municipal Projects:** Municipality of Baie Verte holds authority for municipal governance and infrastructure
**Economic Development:** BVP Chamber of Commerce leads non-infrastructure economic development initiatives

## 4. Community Organizations

### Indigenous Stakeholders
**First Nations Communities:** None located on Baie Verte Peninsula
**Indigenous Organizations:** No indigenous economic development organizations or business associations in the region

**Consultation Requirements:**
- **Engagement Required:** Qalipu First Nation Chief and Environmental Advisor
- **Trigger:** Projects with potential impact on environment or natural resources
- **Scope:** Economic development initiatives that involve environmental or resource impacts

**Strategic Implications:**
- Economic development initiatives may require Qalipu consultation
- Environmental aspects of infrastructure development likely need consultation
- Need to include indigenous consultation in project planning

---

## 5. Regional Stakeholders

### Neighboring Communities
**Regional Context:** 21 communities on Baie Verte Peninsula
**Collaboration Opportunity:** Some potential for regional collaboration
**Regionalization Resistance:** Communities want to remain independent and maintain their own identity
**Competition Dynamics:** Historic competition based on independence and identity, not service contracts

### Regional Economic Development Organizations
**Green Bay-White Bay CBDC:** Exists but low activity
**Baie Verte Regional Economic Development Association:** Currently without a board (inactive)
**Task Force Role:** Fills critical gap in regional coordination

**Strategic Implications:**
- Focus on specific collaboration opportunities while respecting community independence
- Task Force provides essential coordination function
- Regional cooperation possible but must respect independence preferences

## 8. Risk Assessment & Mitigation

### High-Risk Stakeholder Areas

2. **Regional Coordination:** Insufficient respect for community independence
3. **Government Relations:** Inadequate utilization of existing relationships
4. **Business Communication:** Breakdown in direct communication with Firefly

### Mitigation Strategies
1. **Proactive Indigenous Engagement:** Include Qalipu consultation in project planning
2. **Respect Regional Independence:** Focus on specific collaboration opportunities
3. **Leverage Existing Relationships:** Maximize use of established government connections
4. **Maintain Direct Communication:** Preserve CAO-Firefly communication channel
