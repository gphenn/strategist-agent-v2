# Persona Handoff Validation Checklist

## Purpose

Validate that persona handoffs transfer sufficient context, knowledge, and decision rationale so the next persona can proceed with full understanding and confidence.

## What Gets Validated

**Template Reference:** `templates/persona-handoff-template.md`

**Used In:** At every phase transition (after each of 7 phases)

**Audience:** Outgoing and incoming personas, orchestrator, continuity leads

---

## Blocking Criteria (All Required)

### 1. Work Completed in Phase Is Documented ✓

- [ ] **Phase deliverables are clearly stated**
  - What was the phase objective?
  - What were the deliverables?
  - Are all deliverables documented?
  - Is completion status clear?

- [ ] **Methods and approaches are documented**
  - How was the work conducted?
  - What methodology was used?
  - What tools/resources were employed?
  - Could it be replicated?

- [ ] **Quality of work is assessed**
  - Is work production quality good?
  - Did work meet phase success criteria?
  - Are there known gaps or limitations?
  - Would work pass review?

**What This Looks Like:**

```
PHASE DELIVERABLES:

DEFINE Phase (Strategic Analyst → Strategic Specialist):

Deliverables Completed:
1. Strategic Context Brief
   - Status: COMPLETE
   - Quality: Ready for use (meets template requirements)
   - Key findings: Problem clear; scope defined; success criteria stated

2. Problem Definition Document
   - Status: COMPLETE
   - Quality: Comprehensive; multi-stakeholder input
   - Key findings: Root problem identified; not just symptoms

3. Stakeholder Map
   - Status: COMPLETE
   - Quality: Identifies influencers and blockers
   - Key findings: 12 key stakeholders identified; roles clear

Methodology Used:
- Stakeholder interviews (15 conducted)
- Business intelligence analysis
- Data gathering (historical performance, market context)
- Problem structuring workshop

Quality Assessment:
✓ All deliverables meet DEFINE phase completion checklist
✓ Stakeholder feedback is positive
✓ Problem definition is crisp and specific
✓ Scope is clearly bounded
✓ Ready for SPLIT phase work
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ❌ Exhaustive documentation
- ❌ All supporting materials
- ❌ Perfect quality (good enough is sufficient)
- ❌ Unanimous satisfaction

---

### 2. Key Findings & Insights Are Clearly Stated ✓

- [ ] **Major findings are summarized**
  - What were the key discoveries?
  - What's surprising or important?
  - What would someone need to know?
  - Are findings well-documented?

- [ ] **Findings are supported by evidence**
  - Is evidence cited for each finding?
  - How confident are we in findings?
  - What's uncertain or needs validation?
  - Are confidence levels stated?

- [ ] **Implications are clear**
  - What do findings mean?
  - What should next phase do with findings?
  - What assumptions are they based on?
  - What should incoming persona know?

**What This Looks Like:**

```
KEY FINDINGS HANDOFF:

Finding 1: Market Gap Exists and Is Material
Summary: Strategic positioning created SMB market gap; competitors filling
Evidence: 
  - Customer interviews: 80% cited pricing/feature mismatch
  - Competitive analysis: 3 competitors entered SMB space last 18 months
  - Market data: $500M opportunity in SMB segment untapped
Confidence: HIGH (multiple validation sources)

Implications for SPLIT:
- Problem decomposition should focus on gap dynamics
- Root cause analysis should explore positioning vs. market fit
- Hypotheses should test whether gap is defensible or fixable

Finding 2: Vertical Expertise Could Be Differentiator
Summary: Competitors are horizontal; vertical positioning is unclaimed
Evidence:
  - Customer interviews: Automotive customers mention need for industry expertise
  - Competitive benchmarking: No competitor owns vertical positioning
  - Market research: Vertical SaaS companies command premium pricing
Confidence: MEDIUM-HIGH (limited customer data; indirect evidence)

Implications for SPLIT:
- Hypothesis testing should validate willingness to pay for vertical expertise
- Should test whether vertical focus is sustainable competitive advantage

Finding 3: Timeline for Action Is Tight
Summary: Competitive window is 12-18 months; consolidation is happening
Evidence:
  - Competitive intelligence: Market is consolidating
  - Customer feedback: Sense of urgency from prospects
  - Market trends: Fast-moving market dynamics
Confidence: MEDIUM-HIGH (observable trends; some inference)

Implications for SPLIT:
- Need to design solution that can launch quickly
- Rapid execution is critical success factor
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ❌ Every finding detailed
- ❌ Perfect evidence documentation
- ❌ Exhaustive implications
- ❌ Consensus on interpretation

---

### 3. Decisions Made in Phase Are Documented ✓

- [ ] **Major decisions are recorded**
  - What decisions were made?
  - Who made them?
  - What was the rationale?
  - When were they made?

- [ ] **Decision alternatives were considered**
  - What alternatives did we consider?
  - Why were they rejected?
  - What would have been different?
  - Are trade-offs documented?

- [ ] **Decision implications are clear**
  - How do decisions affect next phase?
  - What constraints do they create?
  - What opportunities do they enable?
  - Should incoming persona reconsider?

**What This Looks Like:**

```
DECISIONS MADE IN DEFINE PHASE:

Decision 1: Define Problem as Strategic Positioning Gap (Not Symptom)
Rationale: Customers weren't churning due to product quality or support; 
           they were churning because our positioning didn't address their needs
Alternatives Considered:
  - Frame as "pricing problem" → Too narrow; doesn't capture full issue
  - Frame as "execution problem" → Wrong root cause
Decision Maker: Strategic Analyst + Executive Sponsor
Timeline: Week 3 of DEFINE phase
Implications for SPLIT: Root cause analysis should explore positioning dynamics

Decision 2: Scope to 3 Verticals (Not All SMB)
Rationale: SMB market too broad; better to focus and dominate
Alternatives Considered:
  - Horizontal SMB approach → Too competitive; low margins
  - Single vertical initially → Too limiting; not enough market
Decision Maker: Strategic Analyst + Product Lead
Timeline: Week 4 of DEFINE phase
Implications for SPLIT: Hypothesis testing should validate if 3 verticals is optimal

Decision 3: Use Persona Handoff Approach for Knowledge Transfer
Rationale: Ensure knowledge doesn't get lost between phases
Alternatives Considered:
  - Single person leading all phases → Creates bottleneck
  - No structured handoff → Risk of context loss
Decision Maker: Orchestrator
Timeline: Week 1 of DEFINE phase
Implications for SPLIT: Specialist should use handoff to understand context
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ❌ Every decision documented
- ❌ Perfect alternative analysis
- ❌ Exhaustive implications
- ❌ Immutable decisions (can be revisited)

---

### 4. Alternative Approaches & Rejected Options Documented ✓

- [ ] **Alternatives that were considered**
  - What other approaches were explored?
  - Why were they rejected?
  - What would have been different?
  - Are trade-offs explicit?

- [ ] **Reasoning is clear**
  - Why did we choose this over that?
  - What were the deciding factors?
  - Would someone understand reasoning?
  - Are assumptions stated?

- [ ] **Path not taken is documented**
  - What did we not do?
  - Why not?
  - Could we revisit that path?
  - What would trigger reconsideration?

**What This Looks Like:**

```
ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED & REJECTED:

Alternative 1: Address as Pricing Problem
Approach: Lower pricing to be competitive with SMB vendors
Rationale for rejection:
  - Interviews showed pricing wasn't the only issue
  - Feature gaps and positioning mismatch were equally important
  - Lowering price doesn't solve positioning problem
  - Would destroy enterprise premium positioning
What we learned: Problem is more complex than pricing; requires strategic response

Alternative 2: Serve SMB with Enterprise Product
Approach: Market existing enterprise product to SMB segment
Rationale for rejection:
  - Enterprise product too complex for SMB needs
  - Implementation time too long (120 days vs. needed 30 days)
  - Price point too high for SMB willing to pay
  - Would confuse brand (enterprise vs. SMB)
What we learned: Need separate product strategy; not just marketing adaptation

Alternative 3: Partner/Resell SMB Solution
Approach: Partner with SMB-focused vendor; white-label their solution
Rationale for rejection:
  - Loss of control over product roadmap
  - Customer relationships owned by partner
  - Revenue sharing reduces profitability
  - Strategic dependency on partner
What we learned: Need own product to maintain control and defensibility

Path Not Taken:
If we had pursued pricing-only approach, we would have:
- Missed root cause (positioning, not price)
- Damaged enterprise business
- Not addressed real customer need
Trigger for reconsideration: If vertical expertise strategy proves infeasible
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ❌ Every alternative considered
- ❌ Perfect analysis of paths not taken
- ❌ Certainty we chose correctly
- ❌ Immutable rejection (can revisit)

---

### 5. Assumptions & Uncertainties Are Surfaced ✓

- [ ] **Key assumptions underlying work are identified**
  - What assumptions did we make?
  - Why did we make them?
  - How confident are we?
  - What if they're wrong?

- [ ] **Uncertainties are documented**
  - What don't we know?
  - What needs validation?
  - What could surprise us?
  - What should next phase test?

- [ ] **Risk to assumptions is assessed**
  - If assumptions are wrong, how bad is it?
  - What assumptions are critical?
  - Which should be tested immediately?
  - Which can be validated later?

**What This Looks Like:**

```
ASSUMPTIONS & UNCERTAINTIES HANDOFF:

Assumption 1: Strategic Positioning Created the Gap
Confidence: HIGH (validated through customer interviews)
Evidence: 80% of customers cited positioning/feature mismatch
Risk if wrong: Problem might be unsolvable (if root cause different)
Testing needed: Minimal (well validated); could be revisited if market doesn't respond

Assumption 2: Market Will Value Vertical Expertise
Confidence: MEDIUM (limited data; mostly inferred)
Evidence: 3 customers said they'd pay premium; no transaction evidence
Risk if wrong: SMB strategy doesn't work; wrong go-to-market
Testing needed: Hypothesis testing in SPLIT phase (validate willingness to pay)

Assumption 3: Can Build and Launch SMB Product in 12 Months
Confidence: MEDIUM (never done SMB product before)
Evidence: Industry benchmarks; comparable company examples
Risk if wrong: Timeline slips; competitive window closes
Testing needed: Product feasibility study; detailed planning in SPLIT phase

Uncertainty 1: Actual Customer Adoption Curves Unknown
Issue: Don't have real SMB customer adoption data
Impact: Financial models are estimates; actual ramp could be different
Testing needed: Validate in pilot phase

Uncertainty 2: Competitive Response Timing Unknown
Issue: Don't know when/how competitors will respond
Impact: Window for market entry unclear
Testing needed: Continuous competitive intelligence; scenario planning

Uncertainty 3: Product Differentiation Durability Unknown
Issue: Vertical expertise could be copied; sustainability unclear
Impact: Advantage could be temporary
Testing needed: Competitive analysis; strategic defensibility assessment

Critical Assumptions for SPLIT Phase:
1. Vertical market will pay premium (should validate immediately)
2. Competitors can't match our approach easily (monitor continuously)
3. Market gap is fixable via new product (test through hypothesis)
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ❌ Every assumption listed
- ❌ Perfect confidence assessment
- ❌ All uncertainties addressed
- ❌ Elimination of uncertainty

---

### 6. Handoff Document Is Clear & Usable ✓

- [ ] **Document is well-organized**
  - Can next persona understand it?
  - Is structure logical?
  - Easy to find information?
  - Could be presented/discussed?

- [ ] **Key information is highlighted**
  - What does next persona need to know?
  - Most important items stand out?
  - Could understand in 30 minutes?
  - Gets to key points quickly?

- [ ] **Handoff communication is scheduled**
  - Is transfer meeting scheduled?
  - Will personas discuss live?
  - Are questions addressed?
  - Is clarity confirmed?

**What This Looks Like:**

```
HANDOFF DOCUMENT STRUCTURE:

1-Page Executive Handoff:
- Phase completed: DEFINE (problem clarification)
- Key finding: Strategic positioning created market gap
- Recommendation: Proceed to SPLIT with gap-focused problem decomposition
- Timeline: Ready for SPLIT phase handoff
- Next persona: Strategic Specialist

Detailed Handoff (3-5 pages):
1. Phase Summary
   - Objectives achieved
   - Deliverables completed
   - Quality assessment

2. Key Findings
   - Major discoveries (with evidence)
   - Confidence levels
   - Implications for next phase

3. Decisions Made
   - Strategic decisions documented
   - Alternatives considered
   - Rationale for choices

4. Assumptions & Uncertainties
   - Key assumptions stated
   - Confidence levels
   - What should be tested next

5. Questions for Next Persona
   - What SPLIT phase should explore
   - What hypotheses to test
   - What would change direction

Handoff Meeting:
- Duration: 1-2 hours
- Attendees: Outgoing persona, incoming persona, orchestrator
- Agenda: Review handoff; address questions; confirm understanding
- Outcome: Incoming persona confident to proceed

Post-Handoff:
- Handoff document archived
- Questions documented
- Follow-up items assigned
- Next persona ready to start
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ❌ Polished presentation
- ❌ Perfect documentation
- ❌ Extensive supporting materials
- ❌ Live video recording

---

## Quality Checks (Before Approving)

1. **Would incoming persona understand context?**
   - Could they proceed with confidence?
   - Would they know what to do?
   - Do they have sufficient information?

2. **Are key insights documented?**
   - Most important findings included?
   - Evidence provided?
   - Implications explained?

3. **Are uncertainties clear?**
   - What needs to be tested?
   - What assumptions need validation?
   - What could change direction?

4. **Is handoff well-communicated?**
   - Is document clear?
   - Will meeting transfer knowledge?
   - Would questions be answered?

---

## Readiness Assessment

### ✅ Handoff is Ready When

All blocking criteria are met:
- Work completed is documented
- Key findings clearly stated
- Decisions made are recorded
- Alternatives/rejected options documented
- Assumptions and uncertainties surfaced
- Document is clear and usable

**Incoming persona can proceed with full understanding.**
