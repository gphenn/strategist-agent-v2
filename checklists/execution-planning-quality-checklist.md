# Execution Planning Quality Checklist

## Purpose

Validate that execution plans are detailed, sequenced logically, have clear ownership, and are realistic for implementation. Ensure plans can successfully guide execution from start to finish.

## What Gets Validated

**Task Reference:** `tasks/execution-planning.md`

**Used In:** ACT phase for translating strategy to action

**Audience:** Execution leaders, project managers, team leads

---

## Blocking Criteria (All Required)

### 1. Plan Has Clear Objectives & Success Criteria âœ“

- [ ] **What success looks like is defined**
  - What are we trying to achieve?
  - What's the end goal?
  - How will we measure success?
  - What's done/acceptable?

- [ ] **Success metrics are specific**
  - Financial metrics (revenue, profitability)?
  - Operational metrics (customers, velocity)?
  - Quality metrics (satisfaction, churn)?
  - Timeline metrics (deadline)?

- [ ] **Objectives are aligned with strategy**
  - Do plan objectives match strategic decision?
  - Are we moving toward the goal?
  - Are we solving the right problem?
  - Does execution serve strategy?

**What This Looks Like:**

```
OBJECTIVES & SUCCESS CRITERIA:

Strategic Decision: Launch SMB product offering

Plan Objective:
Deliver viable SMB product to market and establish market foothold
with 10+ customers in first 12 months.

Success Criteria:

Financial Metrics:
- $500K+ ARR by Month 12 (revenue target)
- Gross margin 75%+ (profitability target)
- Customer CAC <$50K (efficiency target)

Operational Metrics:
- 10+ customers by Month 12 (adoption target)
- <45 day implementation (speed target)
- 3+ product releases (velocity target)

Quality Metrics:
- Customer NPS 50+ (satisfaction target)
- <8% churn (retention target)
- 95%+ uptime (reliability target)

Timeline:
- MVP ready Month 6 (product target)
- Pilot customers live Month 8 (validation target)
- Market launch Month 10 (launch target)
- Target profitability Month 18 (financial target)

Overall Success Definition:
Product is viable (NPS 50+), adopted (10+ customers),
efficient (CAC <$50K), and on path to profitability.
```

- âŒ Perfect metrics
- âŒ Quantified for every goal

### 2. Plan Has Detailed Sequence & Milestones âœ“

- [ ] **Work is sequenced logically**
  - Do tasks happen in right order?
  - Are dependencies clear?
  - Would someone know what to do?
  - Are critical path items identified?

- [ ] **Milestones are meaningful**
  - What are the key checkpoints?
  - When does each milestone occur?
  - What indicates milestone completion?
  - Are milestones realistic?

- [ ] **Timeline is realistic**
  - Is 18-month plan credible?
  - Are effort estimates reasonable?
  - Is schedule achievable?
  - What are the risks to timeline?

**What This Looks Like:**

```
EXECUTION PLAN SEQUENCE:

Phase 1: Foundation (Month 1-2)
- Hire product lead for SMB (critical path)
- Define SMB product scope (depends on lead hire)
- Build development team (parallel to scope)
- Establish SMB GTM strategy (parallel)
Milestone: Team in place; scope locked

Phase 2: Development (Month 3-6)
- Build core product features (critical path)
- Develop implementation playbook (parallel)
- Establish support process (parallel)
- Conduct 3 pilot customer interviews (parallel)
Milestone: MVP ready for pilot testing

Phase 3: Pilot (Month 7-9)
- Recruit 3-5 pilot customers (depends on product ready)
- Implement with pilots (depends on product ready)
- Gather feedback; refine product (depends on implementation)
- Train sales team (parallel)
Milestone: Pilots live; feedback gathered

Phase 4: Launch (Month 10-12)
- Finalize product based on pilot learning (depends on pilot feedback)
- Launch market GTM campaign (depends on product ready)
- Acquire first customers (depends on GTM launch)
- Measure results; optimize (parallel)
Milestone: 5-10 customers acquired; $500K ARR on track

Phase 5: Scale (Month 13-18)
- Acquire additional customers (depends on GTM working)
- Build customer success function (depends on customer growth)
- Optimize product based on customer feedback (parallel)
- Prepare for profitability (parallel)
Milestone: 10+ customers; on track to profitability

Critical Path:
Product lead hire â†’ Product scope â†’ Development â†’ MVP â†’
Pilot feedback â†’ Product refinement â†’ Launch â†’ Customer acquisition
```

- âŒ Gantt chart with all details

### 3. Plan Has Clear Ownership & Accountability âœ“

- [ ] **Owner assigned to each major workstream**
  - Product owner: Accountable for product
  - GTM owner: Accountable for sales/marketing
  - Operations owner: Accountable for support/systems
  - Executive sponsor: Accountable for overall execution

- [ ] **Roles & responsibilities are clear**
  - What is each person accountable for?
  - Who makes decisions in each area?
  - Who reports progress?
  - Who has authority to make changes?

- [ ] **Decision rights are clear**
  - Who decides when things change?
  - Who escalates issues?
  - Who commits resources?
  - Who decides go/no-go?

**What This Looks Like:**

```
OWNERSHIP & ACCOUNTABILITY:

Product Workstream:
- Owner: VP Product (hired new, SMB expert)
- Accountability: Product ready by Month 6; NPS 50+ by Month 12
- Team: 1 PM + 3-4 engineers + 1 QA
- Decisions: Feature prioritization, architecture, tradeoffs
- Reporting: Weekly to Executive Sponsor

GTM Workstream:
- Owner: VP Sales (expanded role from enterprise)
- Accountability: 10+ customers by Month 12; CAC <$50K
- Team: 1 Account executive + 1 Sales development + marketing
- Decisions: Customer targeting, pricing, partnership
- Reporting: Weekly to Executive Sponsor

Operations Workstream:
- Owner: VP Customer Success (new function)
- Accountability: Implementation <45 days; churn <8%; NPS 50+
- Team: 1 Customer success manager + 1 Support person
- Decisions: Process, tools, customer success approach
- Reporting: Weekly to Executive Sponsor

Executive Sponsor:
- Owner: Chief Product Officer
- Accountability: Overall execution; timeline; budget
- Authority: Remove blockers; adjust resources; make escalation calls
- Reporting: Monthly to CEO; quarterly to Board

Decision Rights:
- Feature changes: VP Product (under budget constraints)
- Customer changes: VP Sales (with VP Product input)
- Process changes: VP CS (with support from ops)
- Budget/resource changes: CFO (with Executive Sponsor approval)
- Strategic pivots: CEO (with Executive Sponsor recommendation)
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- âŒ Detailed org chart
- âŒ Full RACI matrix
- âŒ Extensive governance structure
- âŒ Unanimous ownership clarity

### 5. Plan Identifies & Addresses Key Risks âœ“

- [ ] **Major risks are identified**
  - What could derail execution?
  - What's the biggest risk?
  - What external factors could impact?
  - Are obvious risks included?

- [ ] **Risk mitigation is outlined**
  - How would we prevent key risks?
  - How would we detect early?
  - How would we respond?
  - Is mitigation realistic?

- [ ] **Contingency plans exist**
  - If timeline slips, what do we do?
  - If customer adoption slower, what do we do?
  - If team member leaves, what do we do?
  - Are alternatives identified?

**What This Looks Like:**

```
RISK MANAGEMENT:

Risk 1: Product Development Timeline Slips

Risk: Development takes longer than 6 months

Mitigation:
- Hire experienced VP Product (knows how to execute fast)
- Reduce scope to must-haves only
- Weekly milestone tracking
- Resource contingency built in

Detection:
- Monthly milestone reviews (Is product on track?)
- If 2+ weeks behind, escalate
- Trigger: Month 4 milestone slipped

Response:
- Reduce scope further (cut lower-priority features)
- Add resources if available
- Delay launch 2-4 weeks (manage expectations)
- Descope 2-3 features to stay close to timeline

Residual risk: Acceptable (could live with Month 8 launch)

---

Risk 2: Pilot Customers Don't Adopt/Churn

Risk: Pilots show low satisfaction (NPS <40) or churn

Mitigation:
- Carefully select pilot customers (strong fit)
- Dedicated support during pilot
- Weekly check-ins with pilots
- Rapid iteration based on feedback

Detection:
- Monthly pilot satisfaction tracking
- NPS target Month 8: 50+
- If NPS <40, pause additional pilots

Response:
- Pause new customer acquisitions
- Focus on pilot customer success
- Product refinement based on feedback
- 4-week re-engagement effort
- If still <40 NPS after refinement, reconsider market fit

Residual risk: Acceptable (market could be wrong; would pivot)

---

Risk 3: Key Talent Leaves

Risk: VP Product or key engineer departs mid-project

Mitigation:
- Competitive compensation (top-of-market pay)
- Clear equity/incentives for retention
- Strong vision/mission (people motivated)
- Succession plan in place

Detection:
- Regular 1:1s with key people
- Manager check-ins on engagement
- Pulse surveys quarterly

Response:
- Emergency backfill (recruitment)
- Interim coverage (other team members)
- Compressed timeline (if necessary)
- Scope adjustment (if necessary)

Residual risk: Manageable (could impact timeline; not fatal)

---

Risk 4: Faster Competitor Response

Risk: Competitor launches SMB product Month 8 (before us)

Mitigation:
- Move fast (hit Month 10 launch target)
- Differentiate via vertical expertise
- Build customer relationships early (pilots become advocates)
- Lock in early customers (incentivize commitment)

Detection:
- Weekly competitive intelligence
- If competitor announces, assess threat
- Re-evaluate positioning if needed

Response:
- Accelerate differentiation (enhance vertical focus)
- Emphasize customer success (switching cost)
- Consider partnership/acquisition (if strategic fit)

Residual risk: Acceptable (18-month lead is still defensible)
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- âŒ Exhaustive risk list
- âŒ Perfect risk mitigation
- âŒ Zero contingency
- âŒ Risk-free execution

### 6. Plan Includes Communication & Governance âœ“

- [ ] **Stakeholder communication is planned**
  - Who needs updates?
  - How frequently?
  - What format?
  - What's the escalation path?

- [ ] **Progress tracking is defined**
  - How will we measure progress?
  - What metrics matter?
  - How frequently reviewed?
  - Who tracks and reports?

- [ ] **Decision governance is clear**
  - How are go/no-go decisions made?
  - When do we assess progress?
  - Who has authority?
  - What's the escalation process?

**What This Looks Like:**

```
COMMUNICATION & GOVERNANCE:

Stakeholder Communication:

Executive Sponsor (CPO):
- Frequency: Weekly
- Format: 30-min status meeting
- Topics: Milestones, risks, decisions needed
- Owner: Workstream leads rotate

Board:
- Frequency: Quarterly
- Format: 15-min board update
- Topics: Progress, key milestones, financials
- Owner: Executive Sponsor

Team:
- Frequency: Weekly
- Format: 1-hour all-hands meeting
- Topics: Progress, blockers, celebrations
- Owner: Executive Sponsor

Progress Tracking:

Weekly Metrics:
- Product: Feature completion %, bugs, velocity
- GTM: Pipeline, customer interest
- Ops: Support ticket volume, response time

Monthly Metrics:
- Product: Milestone achievement, team morale
- GTM: Conversation rate, deal pipeline
- Ops: Implementation time, customer NPS

Quarterly Metrics:
- Financial: Budget vs. actual, ARR trajectory
- Operational: Customer count, churn, retention
- Strategic: Competitive positioning, market feedback

Governance:

Monthly Executive Review:
- Review progress vs. plan
- Assess financials
- Review key risks
- Make go/no-go decisions
- Adjust plan if needed

Quarterly Board Review:
- High-level progress update
- Financial performance
- Key risks and mitigation
- Forward guidance

Go/No-Go Decision Points:
- Month 6: Product ready for pilot? (GO/NO-GO)
- Month 9: Pilot results positive? (GO/NO-GO)
- Month 12: Customer acquisition on track? (GO/NO-GO)
- Month 18: Profitability path clear? (GO/NO-GO)

Escalation Process:
- Issue identified by workstream lead
- Escalated to Executive Sponsor if can't resolve
- Executive Sponsor brings to monthly review
- If major: CEO and board notified
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- âŒ Perfect communication cadence
- âŒ Formal governance structure
- âŒ Multi-tier reporting
- âŒ Real-time dashboards

## Quality Checks (Before Approving)

Before confirming plan is complete, ask yourself:

1. **Is plan understandable?**
   - Could someone execute from this plan?
   - Are tasks and dependencies clear?
   - Would execution team know what to do?

2. **Is plan realistic?**
   - Are estimates reasonable?
   - Is timeline credible?
   - Is team sized right?
   - Are risks acknowledged?

3. **Does plan align with strategy?**
   - Are plan objectives aligned with decision?
   - Does execution serve the goal?
   - Would success achieve the outcome?

4. **Are we ready to execute?**
   - Are resources committed?
   - Is budget approved?
   - Is team in place?
   - Are we ready to start?

## Post-Validation Actions

Once this checklist passes:

1. **Get Executive Approval**
   - Present plan to leadership
   - Address questions/concerns
   - Confirm resource commitments
   - Authorize execution

2. **Prepare Execution Team**
   - Share plan with full team
   - Clarify roles and responsibilities
   - Confirm understanding
   - Answer questions

3. **Set Up Governance**
   - Schedule regular reviews
   - Create progress tracking system
   - Establish communication cadence
   - Prepare materials/templates

4. **Begin Execution**
   - Month 1 activities start
   - Progress tracking begins
   - Regular governance meetings start
   - Execution underway
