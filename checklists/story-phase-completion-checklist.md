# STORY Phase Completion Checklist

## Purpose

Validate that the STORY phase (Strategic Communicator) has completed all essential work before transitioning to the DECIDE phase. This checklist ensures strategic insights have been transformed into compelling narratives that inform and influence stakeholder thinking about critical strategic decisions.

## What This Phase Accomplishes

The STORY phase makes strategic insights accessible and persuasive. The Strategic Communicator ensures:
- Complex analysis becomes compelling narrative
- Insights are tailored to different stakeholders
- Case for action is compelling
- Key messages are consistent across audiences
- Foundation for decision-making is solid

## Blocking Criteria (All Required)

### 1. Core Strategic Narrative Developed ‚úì

- [ ] **Master narrative created**
  - Clear story arc (current state ‚Üí challenge ‚Üí analysis ‚Üí insight ‚Üí choice point)
  - Core message repeated throughout
  - Evidence woven into story (not appended as separate analysis)
  - Emotional resonance with audience
  - Call-to-action (what decision/action is being informed)

- [ ] **Narrative logic is clear**
  - Each element builds on previous
  - Why we did certain analyses is clear
  - Why certain findings matter is explained
  - Implications are front-and-center (not buried)
  - Path to decision is visible

- [ ] **Narrative is evidence-based**
  - Claims are supported by findings
  - Supporting data provided
  - Caveats and uncertainties acknowledged
  - Tone is credible (not overselling)

**What This Looks Like:**
```
NARRATIVE ARC:
Current State: Enterprise success, SMB challenges
Challenge: Market opportunity vs. resource constraints
Analysis: Deep dive into churn, competition, opportunity
Insight: Double-sided growth opportunity (enterprise + SMB)
Choice Point: Do we defend premium or expand downmarket?
Call-to-Action: Strategic decision needed by [date]
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Perfect storytelling
- ‚ùå Polished presentation
- ‚ùå Unanimous agreement with narrative
- ‚ùå Final decision on what to do

---

### 2. Stakeholder Narratives Customized ‚úì

- [ ] **Key stakeholder groups identified**
  - Executive leadership
  - Board of directors (if relevant)
  - Operational leadership (Sales, Product, Ops)
  - Affected teams/departments
  - External stakeholders (if relevant)

- [ ] **Customized narratives for each group**
  - For executives: Strategic implications and business impact
  - For operations: What this means for their areas, what changes
  - For board: Market opportunity, competitive position, risk management
  - For affected teams: How they're impacted, what they need to do
  - For external: Opportunity/threat relevant to their interests

- [ ] **Core message is consistent across audiences**
  - Different narratives tell same basic story
  - Supporting evidence is consistent
  - Tone is appropriate for each audience
  - No contradictions between narratives

**What This Looks Like:**
- **For CEO:** "Strategic choice between defending premium positioning or capturing growing SMB market; decision affects 3-year roadmap"
- **For Sales:** "Competitor moving into SMB segment aggressively; we're at risk of losing mid-market customers; strategic response being formulated"
- **For Product:** "Analysis shows feature gaps vs. competitors; two possible strategic responses (premium features for enterprise OR simplified feature set for SMB); strategic direction needed"
- **For Board:** "Market analysis shows $500M opportunity in adjacent segment; current strategy captures enterprise (stable, profitable); opportunity to expand total addressable market"

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Perfect tailoring for every individual
- ‚ùå Handling of all possible stakeholder concerns
- ‚ùå Comprehensive internal communication plan
- ‚ùå Defined messaging hierarchy

---

### 3. Key Messages & Messaging Framework ‚úì

- [ ] **Core key messages defined (3-5 primary)**
  - Each message is simple (1-2 sentences max)
  - Each message is memorable
  - Messages are consistent across narratives
  - Messages are backed by evidence
  - Messages point toward decision/action

- [ ] **Message architecture built**
  - Primary message (the headline)
  - Supporting messages (evidence/reasoning)
  - Alternative perspectives acknowledged
  - Counterarguments addressed
  - Call-to-action clear

- [ ] **Supporting evidence for each message**
  - Data points supporting each message
  - Real quotes or examples (if available)
  - Visualizations supporting key points
  - Confidence levels transparent

**What This Looks Like:**
```
PRIMARY MESSAGE:
"We have a strategic choice: focus exclusively on profitable
enterprise market OR expand into underserved SMB segment"

SUPPORTING MESSAGES:
1. "Enterprise is stable and highly profitable ($15M ARR, 95% retention)"
2. "SMB segment is in crisis (40% annual churn, $5M ARR at risk)"
3. "Market data shows SMB segment is large ($2B) and growing 25% annually"
4. "Competitors are moving downmarket aggressively"
5. "We can serve both with different product/pricing models"

EVIDENCE:
- Financial: ARR by segment, retention by segment
- Market: TAM analysis, competitor positioning
- Customer: Churn analysis, NPS by segment
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Every possible supporting data point
- ‚ùå Perfect message wording
- ‚ùå Unanimous stakeholder agreement
- ‚ùå Final positioning statement

---

### 4. Executive Communication Ready ‚úì

- [ ] **Executive summary/brief prepared**
  - 2-3 page document (maximum)
  - Situation, findings, implications stated
  - Strategic choice articulated
  - Recommendations or questions for decision
  - Next steps clear

- [ ] **Presentation structure planned**
  - Opening that engages
  - Problem/opportunity clearly set up
  - Evidence presented logically
  - Implications explained
  - Decision points/options outlined
  - Call-to-action clear
  - Q&A preparation (likely questions anticipated)

- [ ] **Visuals planned**
  - Key charts/visuals identified
  - Data visualization strategy
  - Not too many (focus on key points)
  - Visuals tell story (not just pretty)

**What This Looks Like:**
- **2-page Brief:**
  - Page 1: Situation, challenge, key findings
  - Page 2: Implications, strategic choice, next steps

- **15-minute Presentation:**
  - Opening (30 sec): Business situation
  - Problem (2 min): What's happening with churn
  - Analysis (5 min): Why it's happening
  - Insight (4 min): What it means strategically
  - Choice (2 min): What decision needs to be made
  - Path Forward (1.5 min): What we do next

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Polished PowerPoint
- ‚ùå Complete presentation (outline/script sufficient)
- ‚ùå Every possible visual
- ‚ùå Perfect timing

---

### 5. Alternative Perspectives Addressed ‚úì

- [ ] **Different viewpoints acknowledged**
  - "Some might argue..." perspectives identified
  - Why different stakeholders might see this differently
  - Legitimate concerns surfaced
  - Not dismissed or minimized

- [ ] **Counterarguments addressed**
  - What would challenge our narrative?
  - How do we respond to "but what about...?"
  - Weaknesses in our argument acknowledged
  - What would prove us wrong identified

- [ ] **Uncertainty is transparent**
  - What we're confident about stated
  - What we're less certain about identified
  - What could change our mind noted
  - Risk factors acknowledged

**What This Looks Like:**
- **Alternative View 1:** "Maybe SMB churn is temporary and will stabilize"
  - **Response:** "Possible, but trend data shows continued decline; competitor pressure increasing"

- **Alternative View 2:** "Maybe we should just wait and see what happens"
  - **Response:** "Market window may close; competitors moving fast; window of opportunity may be 6-12 months"

- **Uncertainty:** "We're confident about churn drivers; less certain about exact market sizing; would validate through customer research before committing resources"

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Every possible objection addressed
- ‚ùå Consensus on alternative views
- ‚ùå Complete risk mitigation plans
- ‚ùå Resolved uncertainties

---

### 6. Decision Framework for Next Phase ‚úì

- [ ] **Decision points are explicit**
  - What decision(s) need to be made?
  - Who needs to decide?
  - What's the timeline for decision?
  - What information/input is needed for decision?

- [ ] **Decision options are outlined**
  - Option 1 (and implications)
  - Option 2 (and implications)
  - Option 3 if relevant (and implications)
  - What each option would mean for organization/customers/strategy

- [ ] **Criteria for decision are suggested**
  - What factors should inform decision?
  - Market opportunity vs. resource constraints
  - Competitive timing
  - Organizational capability
  - Financial implications

**What This Looks Like:**
```
DECISION: Should we expand to SMB segment?

OPTIONS:
Option 1: Focus exclusively on enterprise
  - Keep current positioning and pricing
  - Implications: Miss $500M market opportunity; defend against competitive downmarket moves

Option 2: Create separate SMB offering
  - New product, pricing, go-to-market
  - Implications: Double R&D effort; new GTM complexity; open two-sided growth

Option 3: Acquire SMB-focused competitor
  - Faster entry; built-in product
  - Implications: Integration risk; management distraction; higher investment

DECISION CRITERIA:
- Market opportunity size and timeline
- Competitive threat timeline
- Resource availability
- Organizational appetite for dual strategies
- Financial ROI/payback
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Recommendation (that's DECIDE phase)
- ‚ùå Detailed business cases (that's DECIDE)
- ‚ùå Final decision (that's executive decision-making)
- ‚ùå Implementation implications (that's ACT)

---

### 7. Documentation & Handoff ‚úì

- [ ] **Strategic narrative document created**
  - Story arc clear
  - Evidence woven in
  - Implications emphasized
  - Decision points articulated
  - 5-10 pages (enough detail to stand alone)

- [ ] **Stakeholder-specific narratives prepared**
  - Executive version (2-3 pages)
  - Operational leadership version (3-4 pages)
  - Board version (if relevant)
  - Team communication version
  - All aligned in core message

- [ ] **Supporting materials compiled**
  - Key charts and visuals
  - Detailed findings reference
  - Message framework document
  - Presentation outline/script

- [ ] **Persona Handoff created**
  - Narrative summary
  - Key decision points
  - Strategic options outlined
  - Questions/concerns for Advisor to address in DECIDE
  - Stakeholder perspectives documented
  - Execution considerations noted

**What This Looks Like:**
- **Narrative Document:** 8-10 page strategic brief with story arc, findings, implications
- **Executive Brief:** 2-3 page summary for busy executives
- **Presentation Outline:** 15-20 minute presentation script with speaker notes
- **Message Framework:** Document with primary message, supporting messages, evidence

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Polished presentations
- ‚ùå Complete stakeholder communication plan
- ‚ùå Final marketing/communications materials
- ‚ùå Resolved stakeholder concerns

## Enhancing Criteria (Improving Quality)

These items strengthen the phase but aren't strictly blocking:

- [ ] **Story tested with key stakeholders**
  - Narrative reviewed with sample of stakeholders
  - Feedback incorporated
  - Resonance assessed
  - Concerns surfaced and addressed

- [ ] **Compelling evidence compilation**
  - Customer stories/quotes included
  - Competitive examples cited
  - Market trend examples provided
  - Emotional resonance created through examples

- [ ] **Visual storyline developed**
  - Charts that tell the story
  - Flow from one visual to next
  - Key insights emphasized visually
  - Professional quality (not required, but helpful)

- [ ] **Presentation rehearsed**
  - Timing verified
  - Likely questions anticipated
  - Transitions smooth
  - Spokesperson confident

- [ ] **Organizational readiness assessed**
  - How will different groups receive this?
  - Are there sensitivities to navigate?
  - Is there organizational capacity for recommended changes?
  - Communication sequencing identified

## Quality Checks (Before Handoff)

Before confirming phase completion, ask yourself:

1. **Is the narrative truly compelling?**
   - Would it move someone to action?
   - Does it resonate emotionally as well as intellectually?
   - Does it make the case for why this matters?
   - Would different stakeholders find it persuasive?

2. **Is the story clear without the analysis?**
   - Could someone understand the logic without detailed data?
   - Do they see why analysis was conducted?
   - Do implications flow logically from findings?
   - Is path to decision clear?

3. **Are stakeholders adequately addressed?**
   - Would each key group understand what this means for them?
   - Are their concerns/perspectives represented?
   - Would they feel heard and understood?
   - Would they be ready to engage in decision?

4. **Does the Communicator feel the story is ready?**
   - Are they confident in the narrative?
   - Can they answer questions about findings?
   - Are they ready to hand off to Advisor?
   - Do they feel the story is compelling?

## Common Blockers & How to Resolve

| Blocker | Signal | Resolution |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Narrative feels data-heavy** | "Too many numbers" | Weave evidence into story; don't append analysis |
| **No emotional resonance** | "It's interesting but who cares?" | Add examples, customer stories, real impact |
| **Too many messages** | "We have 15 key messages" | Ruthlessly consolidate; focus on 3-5 core |
| **Stakeholders misaligned** | "Different groups interpret differently" | Create customized narratives for each |
| **Decision unclear** | "What are we actually deciding?" | Articulate explicitly; outline options |
| **Overcomplicated** | "Too hard to explain" | Simplify story arc; focus on essentials |

## Post-Completion Actions

Once this checklist passes:

1. **Create Strategic Narrative Document**
   - Full story arc documented
   - Evidence woven throughout
   - Implications emphasized
   - Decision points clear

2. **Prepare Executive Summary**
   - 2-3 page brief
   - Situation, findings, implications, decision
   - Presentation-ready
   - Speaker notes included

3. **Compile Stakeholder Narratives**
   - Each key stakeholder group has version
   - Core message consistent
   - Customization relevant
   - All versions coordinated

4. **Create Persona Handoff**
   - Use `persona-handoff-template.md`
   - Narrative summary
   - Decision framework outlined
   - Stakeholder perspectives documented
   - Execution considerations noted

5. **Prepare for Decision Phase**
   - Share narrative documents
   - Share message framework
   - Prepare presentation materials
   - Confirm Advisor ready for DECIDE

6. **Hand off to Strategic Advisor**
   - Share all narrative materials
   - Confirm Advisor ready to facilitate decisions
   - Load DECIDE phase files

7. **Archive STORY Materials**
   - Save all narratives
   - Document stakeholder feedback
   - Reference for future phases
