# DECIDE Phase Completion Checklist

## Purpose

Validate that the DECIDE phase (Strategic Advisor) has completed all essential work before transitioning to the ACT phase. This checklist ensures strategic options have been thoroughly evaluated, trade-offs are understood, and a clear path forward has been established with defined success metrics.

## What This Phase Accomplishes

The DECIDE phase transforms strategic insights and narrative into actionable decisions. The Strategic Advisor ensures:
- Strategic options are clearly defined and evaluated
- Trade-offs are explicitly analyzed
- Risks are assessed and understood
- Recommendations are clear and justified
- Implementation foundation is established

## Blocking Criteria (All Required)

### 1. Strategic Options Fully Defined ‚úì

- [ ] **All viable options identified**
  - Main strategic options articulated (minimum 2, ideally 3)
  - Each option is substantively different (not minor variations)
  - Options cover main strategic choices (not implementation variations)
  - "Do nothing" option explicitly considered

- [ ] **Each option is fully described**
  - What does this option entail? (Clear, specific)
  - Why is this option viable? (Grounded in findings)
  - What would need to happen for this option? (Prerequisites, timing, resources)
  - What does success look like for this option? (Metrics and targets)

- [ ] **Mutually exclusive nature understood**
  - Can we do multiple options simultaneously? (Clarify if yes/no)
  - If exclusive, implications of not choosing option explained
  - If combinations possible, what are they?
  - Timeline for decision (can we revisit later?)

**What This Looks Like:**

**Option 1: Enterprise-Only Focus (Status Quo+)**
- Continue focused on enterprise segment
- Strengthen competitive position through advanced features
- Accept SMB segment loss as competitive cost
- Requires: Enhanced product roadmap, sales focus on enterprise, accept 10-15% churn
- Success: Enterprise ARR $25M by year 3, NPS >80, >90% retention

**Option 2: Dual-Segment Strategy**
- Create separate SMB offering with simplified feature set
- Maintain enterprise excellence
- Capture SMB growth opportunity
- Requires: Parallel product roadmap, dual go-to-market, separate org structure
- Success: Enterprise $25M + SMB $15M by year 3; retention >85% both segments

**Option 3: Acquire SMB Competitor**
- Purchase existing SMB solution
- Integrate into portfolio
- Leverage combined customer base
- Requires: $50M investment, integration team, 12-month timeline
- Success: Combined entity $30M by year 3; cost synergies realized

**Option 4: Do Nothing/Revisit Later**
- Continue current strategy
- Evaluate market conditions in 6-12 months
- Requires: Accept continued churn; monitor competitive moves
- Success: No decision is needed now; revisit timing is clear

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Perfect options (good enough is fine)
- ‚ùå Exhaustive list (main viable options sufficient)
- ‚ùå Complete option specifications (framework sufficient)
- ‚ùå Unanimous agreement that options are right ones

---

### 2. Trade-Off Analysis Completed ‚úì

- [ ] **For each option, trade-offs articulated**
  - What do we gain with this option?
  - What do we give up with this option?
  - Time-based trade-offs (quick wins vs. long-term value)
  - Resource-based trade-offs (investment required, opportunity cost)
  - Risk-based trade-offs (stability vs. opportunity)

- [ ] **Trade-offs compared across options**
  - Option 1 vs. Option 2: What's the difference?
  - Where do options have similar trade-offs?
  - Which option requires most investment?
  - Which option has most risk? Most upside?
  - Is there a trade-off hierarchy (what matters most)?

- [ ] **Strategic priorities inform trade-off assessment**
  - Does organization prioritize profitability or growth?
  - What's appetite for risk/innovation?
  - What timeline matters (short vs. long term)?
  - What's acceptable level of organizational disruption?

**What This Looks Like:**
```
TRADE-OFF MATRIX:

Option 1: Enterprise-Only
Gain: Simplicity, maximize enterprise profitability, lower risk
Give Up: $500M market opportunity, future competitiveness in SMB

Option 2: Dual-Segment
Gain: Capture large market opportunity, future growth, market leadership
Give Up: Profitability (near term), organizational simplicity, R&D complexity

Option 3: Acquire
Gain: Fast entry, built-in SMB product, immediate revenue
Give Up: $50M cash, integration risk, management distraction

TRADE-OFF ASSESSMENT:
If we prioritize: Near-term profit ‚Üí Option 1 wins
If we prioritize: Long-term market share ‚Üí Option 2 wins
If we prioritize: Speed to market ‚Üí Option 3 wins
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Quantified trade-offs (relative assessment sufficient)
- ‚ùå Perfect clarity on all trade-offs
- ‚ùå Consensus on which trade-offs matter most
- ‚ùå Final decision (just clear articulation)

---

### 3. Risk Assessment & Mitigation ‚úì

- [ ] **Risks identified for each option**
  - Strategic risks (market, competitive, execution)
  - Financial risks (investment, ROI)
  - Organizational risks (capability, distraction, culture)
  - Customer risks (satisfaction, retention, churn)
  - Timing risks (market window closing, competitor moves)

- [ ] **Risk probability and impact assessed**
  - Which risks are most likely to occur?
  - Which risks would have biggest negative impact if they occur?
  - High probability + high impact = critical risks
  - Risk ranking established

- [ ] **Mitigation approaches outlined**
  - For critical risks: How would we prevent or mitigate?
  - Mitigation feasibility assessed
  - Mitigation cost considered
  - Residual risk after mitigation understood

- [ ] **Risk monitoring approach suggested**
  - What indicators would signal risk is materializing?
  - How frequently should we monitor?
  - What's the trigger for course correction?
  - Who owns risk monitoring?

**What This Looks Like:**

**Option 2: Dual-Segment Strategy**

**Critical Risks:**
- Risk 1: R&D splits focus; neither product gets best talent
  - Probability: HIGH | Impact: HIGH
  - Mitigation: Establish separate product teams; clear prioritization
  - Residual Risk: MEDIUM (can't completely eliminate)

- Risk 2: SMB sales channel unfamiliar territory; slow ramp
  - Probability: MEDIUM | Impact: MEDIUM
  - Mitigation: Hire experienced SMB sales leader; use partners
  - Residual Risk: MEDIUM

- Risk 3: Competitor launches SMB offering before us
  - Probability: MEDIUM | Impact: HIGH
  - Mitigation: Accelerate timeline; differentiate clearly
  - Residual Risk: MEDIUM

**Monitoring:**
- Quarterly: Competitive landscape review
- Monthly: Product team capacity metrics
- Weekly: Sales pipeline progress
- Trigger: If competitor SMB product found, escalate to executive

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Comprehensive risk register
- ‚ùå Quantified probabilities
- ‚ùå Detailed mitigation plans
- ‚ùå Risk-free options (all have risk)

---

### 4. Financial & Business Case Analysis ‚úì

- [ ] **Financial implications for each option modeled**
  - Investment required (upfront and ongoing)
  - Revenue potential (year 1, 3, 5)
  - Profitability timeline
  - ROI calculation
  - Sensitivity to key assumptions

- [ ] **Financial scenarios considered**
  - Base case (most likely scenario)
  - Upside case (if things go better than expected)
  - Downside case (if things go worse than expected)
  - Break-even analysis

- [ ] **Business case is compelling**
  - Financial returns justify investment/risk
  - ROI timeline is acceptable
  - Value creation is clear
  - Constraints (cash, time, people) respected

**What This Looks Like:**
```
OPTION 2: DUAL-SEGMENT STRATEGY

Investment Required:
- Year 1: $5M (product development, go-to-market)
- Year 2: $3M (continued development, sales ramp)
- Year 3: $2M (optimization)
- Total: $10M

Revenue Potential:
- Enterprise: Grow from $15M to $25M (market growth + share gain)
- SMB: Start $0, grow to $15M by year 3
- Year 1: $16M (enterprise growth + SMB launch)
- Year 3: $40M (full run rate)

Profitability:
- Year 1: Breakeven (investment offsets enterprise margin growth)
- Year 2: $8M profit (enterprise + SMB scaling)
- Year 3: $18M profit (full run-rate profitability)

ROI: 180% cumulative by year 3; payback in 18 months

Key Assumptions:
- SMB TAM is $2B as estimated
- We can capture 2% by year 3
- Product development on schedule
- Sales team can ramp SMB channel
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Detailed financial models
- ‚ùå Perfect forecasting accuracy
- ‚ùå Complex scenario analysis
- ‚ùå DCF or other sophisticated valuation

### 5. Decision Framework Applied ‚úì

- [ ] **Strategic criteria defined for decision**
  - What matters most to this decision? (Growth? Profitability? Risk?)
  - What's alignment with company strategy?
  - What's impact on competitive position?
  - What's organizational readiness?
  - Time horizon that matters (short? medium? long?)

- [ ] **Options evaluated against criteria**
  - Each option scored/ranked against criteria
  - Weighting of criteria applied (if some matter more)
  - How do options compare on each criterion?
  - Where do options differ most?

- [ ] **Recommendation emerges from framework**
  - Based on criteria and framework, which option is strongest?
  - What's the rationale for recommendation?
  - What would need to be true to recommend different option?
  - How confident are we in recommendation?

**What This Looks Like:**
```
STRATEGIC CRITERIA:

1. Market Opportunity (Weight: 30%)
   - Enterprise Only: 20/100 (limited to current market)
   - Dual Segment: 95/100 (captures large SMB opportunity)
   - Acquire: 75/100 (good SMB access, limited upside)

2. Financial Returns (Weight: 25%)
   - Enterprise Only: 80/100 (high margin, steady)
   - Dual Segment: 85/100 (balanced growth/profitability)
   - Acquire: 60/100 (requires large investment)

3. Competitive Position (Weight: 25%)
   - Enterprise Only: 60/100 (vulnerable to downmarket competitor move)
   - Dual Segment: 90/100 (market leadership position)
   - Acquire: 70/100 (established presence, integration risk)

4. Organizational Readiness (Weight: 20%)
   - Enterprise Only: 90/100 (status quo, no disruption)
   - Dual Segment: 65/100 (requires new capabilities, dual focus)
   - Acquire: 50/100 (integration complexity, management distraction)

WEIGHTED SCORES:
- Enterprise Only: 68/100
- Dual Segment: 85/100 ‚Üê RECOMMENDED
- Acquire: 68/100

RECOMMENDATION: Dual-Segment Strategy
RATIONALE: Best balance of growth opportunity, financial return, and competitive position
CONFIDENCE: HIGH (strong on market opportunity; moderate on execution risk)
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Quantified scoring (qualitative okay)
- ‚ùå Complex weighting schemes
- ‚ùå Unanimous agreement with scoring
- ‚ùå Absolute certainty in recommendation

### 6. Implementation Foundations ‚úì

- [ ] **Implementation approach sketched (not detailed)**
  - If we choose this option, how would we execute?
  - Organizational structure implications
  - Timeline estimate (phases, duration)
  - Key milestones/gates
  - Resource requirements (headcount, budget, capabilities)

- [ ] **Success metrics and KPIs defined**
  - What does success look like for chosen option?
  - Financial metrics (revenue, profitability, ROI)
  - Operational metrics (customer satisfaction, retention, growth)
  - Timing (quarterly/annual targets)
  - How will we track progress?

- [ ] **Contingencies and exit ramps identified**
  - What happens if key assumptions prove wrong?
  - When would we consider pivoting?
  - What's the point of no return?
  - What would trigger course correction?

**What This Looks Like:**
```
IMPLEMENTATION SKETCH (Option 2: Dual-Segment):

Phase 1 (Months 1-6): Preparation
- Define SMB product requirements
- Hire SMB product lead and sales lead
- Plan go-to-market strategy
- Budget and resource allocation

Phase 2 (Months 6-12): Product Launch
- Develop SMB feature set (simplified)
- Build SMB go-to-market (partnerships, marketing)
- Launch SMB product to pilot customers
- Establish SMB support/success team

Phase 3 (Months 12-24): Scale
- Ramp SMB sales
- Expand partnership channel
- Optimize product based on early feedback
- Continue enterprise evolution

Phase 4 (Months 24+): Optimize
- SMB reaches $10M+ ARR run rate
- Enterprise reaches $20M+
- Evaluate dual brand vs. integrated approach
- Plan next phase of growth

SUCCESS METRICS:
- Enterprise: $20M ARR by end of Year 2; >90% retention; NPS >75
- SMB: $10M ARR by end of Year 2; >80% retention; NPS >70
- Combined: $40M ARR by end of Year 3; profitability >40%

CONTINGENCIES:
- If SMB adoption slower than expected: Consider acquisition vs. continued organic
- If competitor launches SMB offering: Accelerate launch or revisit enterprise-only
- If org can't handle dual focus: Spin out separate SMB company
- If total available capital insufficient: Phase approach over 24 months instead of 18
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Detailed implementation plans (that's ACT phase)
- ‚ùå Complete resource specifications
- ‚ùå Comprehensive success metrics (directional okay)
- ‚ùå Resolved implementation concerns

---

### 7. Documentation & Clear Recommendation ‚úì

- [ ] **Decision memorandum created**
  - Options articulated
  - Trade-offs analyzed
  - Risk assessment documented
  - Financial case presented
  - Recommendation clear with rationale

- [ ] **Recommendation is clear and defensible**
  - Recommended option stated explicitly
  - Why this option over alternatives (rationale)
  - What's required to execute successfully
  - What could go wrong (risks and mitigations)
  - What success looks like (metrics)

- [ ] **Persona Handoff created**
  - Decision recommendation summarized
  - Strategic rationale explained
  - Implementation approach sketched
  - Key risks and mitigations identified
  - Success metrics and contingencies noted
  - What Innovator needs to focus on (ACT phase)

- [ ] **All supporting materials organized**
  - Decision framework documentation
  - Financial models/projections
  - Risk assessments
  - Stakeholder perspectives documented

**What This Looks Like:**
- **Decision Memo:** 8-10 page document with options, trade-offs, recommendation, rationale
- **Executive Recommendation:** 2-3 page summary with decision and next steps
- **Supporting Materials:** Financial models, risk assessments, implementation sketches

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Polished presentation (word document is fine)
- ‚ùå Unanimous stakeholder agreement
- ‚ùå Detailed implementation plans
- ‚ùå Complete risk mitigation strategies

## Enhancing Criteria (Improving Quality)

These items strengthen the phase but aren't strictly blocking:

- [ ] **Stakeholder perspective integrated**
  - Key stakeholder views on options documented
  - Different perspectives on decision criteria captured
  - How different groups weight criteria noted
  - Build-in points for stakeholder input/agreement

- [ ] **Competitive implications thought through**
  - How will competitors respond to each option?
  - Do any options create competitive vulnerability?
  - Can we defend chosen option competitively?
  - Is there first-mover advantage?

- [ ] **Scenario planning extended**
  - Market upside scenario: How does each option perform?
  - Market downside scenario: What's option resilience?
  - Organizational success scenario: What if execution excellent?
  - Organizational challenge scenario: What if execution difficult?

- [ ] **Quick-win opportunities identified**
  - Within chosen option, what can we start immediately?
  - What can succeed in 30/60/90 days?
  - What builds momentum for larger initiative?
  - What de-risks the larger decision?

## Quality Checks (Before Handoff)

Before confirming phase completion, ask yourself:

1. **Are the options truly strategic choices?**
   - Do they represent meaningfully different directions?
   - Would different stakeholders prefer different options?
   - Is there real trade-off tension between them?
   - Would decision matter significantly to business?

2. **Is the recommendation well-founded?**
   - Would reasonable people understand the logic?
   - Is recommendation defensible against alternatives?
   - What would have to be true for different recommendation?
   - Is confidence level appropriate to decision importance?

3. **Have we addressed key decision concerns?**
   - Would executives understand the options?
   - Would they see the trade-offs clearly?
   - Would they feel confident in recommendation?
   - Would they feel heard and understood?

4. **Is implementation approach realistic?**
   - Would organization be capable of executing?
   - Are resource requirements reasonable?
   - Is timeline realistic?
   - Are contingencies thoughtful?

## Common Blockers & How to Resolve

| Blocker | Signal | Resolution |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Options feel forced** | "These aren't real choices" | Identify truly different strategic paths |
| **No clear winner** | "Options are equally good" | Apply decision framework; weight criteria |
| **Risks overwhelming** | "This is too risky" | Outline mitigation; accept residual risk |
| **Financial case weak** | "The math doesn't work" | Revisit assumptions; consider timing |
| **Recommendation unclear** | "We don't know which to pick" | Apply decision framework explicitly |
| **Implementation daunting** | "How will we ever do this?" | Break into phases; identify quick wins |

## Post-Completion Actions

Once this checklist passes:

1. **Create Decision Memorandum**
   - Options fully described
   - Trade-offs articulated
   - Risk assessment documented
   - Financial case presented
   - Recommendation with rationale

2. **Prepare Executive Summary**
   - 2-3 page recommendation summary
   - Key options and trade-offs
   - Recommendation and next steps

3. **Compile Supporting Materials**
   - Decision framework documentation
   - Financial models/projections
   - Risk assessments and mitigation plans
   - Implementation sketches

4. **Create Persona Handoff**
   - Use `persona-handoff-template.md`
   - Summarize recommendation
   - Outline strategic rationale
   - Sketch implementation approach
   - Highlight key risks
   - Note success metrics and contingencies

5. **Prepare for Implementation Phase**
   - Confirm executive approval of recommendation
   - Brief Innovator on strategic direction
   - Clarify constraints/requirements
   - Prepare for ACT phase

6. **Hand off to Strategic Advisor + Innovator (ACT)**
   - Share decision memorandum
   - Share financial models and risk assessments
   - Confirm team ready for execution planning
   - Load ACT phase files

7. **Archive DECIDE Materials**
   - Save all decision documentation
   - Document decision rationale
   - Reference for future phases
