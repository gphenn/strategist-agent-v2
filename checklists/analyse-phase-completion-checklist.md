# ANALYSE Phase Completion Checklist

## Purpose

Validate that the ANALYSE phase (Strategic Analyst + Strategic Specialist) has completed all essential work before transitioning to the INSIGHT phase. This checklist ensures hypotheses have been systematically tested, data has been rigorously analyzed, and patterns have been identified that support strategic decision-making.

## What This Phase Accomplishes

The ANALYSE phase transforms hypotheses into evidence-based insights. The Analyst and Specialist together ensure:
- All key hypotheses are systematically tested
- Quantitative data supports findings
- Competitive landscape is understood
- Patterns and trends are identified
- Findings are documented with supporting evidence

## Blocking Criteria (All Required)

### 1. Hypothesis Testing Completion âœ“

- [ ] **All priority hypotheses have been tested**
  - High-impact hypotheses: all tested
  - Medium-impact hypotheses: all tested
  - Quick-win hypotheses: tested (or explicitly deferred with reason)
  - At least 80% of hypotheses from SPLIT phase tested

- [ ] **Testing evidence is documented**
  - For each hypothesis: data/evidence gathered
  - Testing methodology documented
  - Sample sizes adequate (or limitations noted)
  - Data sources credible and traceable

- [ ] **Hypotheses validated or refuted**
  - Each hypothesis has clear conclusion: supported, refuted, or inconclusive
  - Strength of evidence assessed (strong, moderate, weak)
  - Confidence level in conclusion noted
  - Boundary conditions identified (when hypothesis holds true)

**What This Looks Like:**
- **Hypothesis:** "SMB customers churn due to pricing"
  - **Testing:** Exit interviews (15 customers), pricing analysis (vs. 5 competitors), market research
  - **Evidence:** 12/15 exit interviews cited price; we're 20-30% higher than closest competitor; market research confirms price sensitivity in SMB
  - **Conclusion:** **STRONGLY SUPPORTED** - price is primary driver for SMB churn
  - **Boundary:** Applies to SMB segment <100 employees; enterprise less price sensitive

- **Hypothesis:** "Product mobile experience drives enterprise churn"
  - **Testing:** Feature gap analysis, NPS by device type, customer interviews
  - **Evidence:** Mobile is 15% of usage; desktop-only features not major complaint; NPS similar across device types
  - **Conclusion:** **REFUTED** - not a primary churn driver; secondary concern
  - **Alternative Found:** Enterprise customers want advanced integrations (API quality)

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- âŒ Statistical significance testing
- âŒ Peer-reviewed academic rigor
- âŒ Testing of tangential hypotheses
- âŒ Proof beyond reasonable doubt (strong evidence is enough)

### 2. Quantitative Analysis âœ“

- [ ] **Financial/business impact quantified**
  - Revenue impact of problem calculated (or estimated with confidence range)
  - Cost impact quantified where relevant
  - Customer impact measured (segment, volume, value)
  - Time-to-impact understood (when does this become critical?)

- [ ] **Market-level analysis completed**
  - Market size (TAM) estimated for relevant segments
  - Market growth rate identified
  - Market trends documented
  - Competitive market dynamics understood

- [ ] **Performance baselines established**
  - Current state metrics captured
  - Industry/competitor benchmarks found
  - Performance gaps quantified
  - Historical trends analyzed (if available)

- [ ] **Financial models created (if applicable)**
  - Cost/benefit models for problem understanding
  - Sensitivity analysis on key variables
  - Scenarios modeled (best/worst/likely case)
  - Assumptions documented and validated

**What This Looks Like:**
- **Financial Impact:** We're losing $5M ARR to churn; competitor pricing costs us 12-15% of SMB revenue; market is growing at 25% annually but we're growing at 8%
- **Market Analysis:** $2B addressable SMB market; 5 major competitors; market consolidating around feature-rich solutions
- **Performance Gap:** We're priced 25% above market average; NPS 15 points below competitors; customer retention is 85% vs. 95% industry average
- **Models:** NPV of $50M opportunity if we reduce churn by 5%; breakeven in 18 months on pricing change

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- âŒ Perfect data
- âŒ Multiple years of trend data
- âŒ Complex financial modeling
- âŒ Competitive intelligence on every competitor

### 3. Competitive Analysis âœ“

- [ ] **Competitive landscape mapped**
  - Direct competitors identified (3-5 main ones)
  - Indirect competitors/substitutes identified
  - Competitive positioning compared (features, pricing, positioning)
  - Competitive advantages and disadvantages assessed

- [ ] **Competitive differentiation understood**
  - Where we're stronger than competitors
  - Where competitors are stronger
  - Defensibility of advantages assessed
  - Gaps in market (unmet needs) identified

- [ ] **Competitive threats and opportunities identified**
  - New competitive threats emerging (or not)
  - Competitive vulnerabilities to exploit
  - Possible competitive responses to our actions
  - Strategic positioning implications

**What This Looks Like:**
- **Competitive Landscape:** 4 main competitors; Company X is market leader (40% share); we're #3 (15% share)
- **Feature Comparison:**
  - Us vs. Competitor X: They have mobile app (we don't); we have better integrations; they're faster to deploy
  - Pricing: We're 25% higher; they have aggressive SMB pricing
- **Differentiation:** Our strength is enterprise features; weakness is SMB pricing; opportunity is integration ecosystem
- **Threat:** Company X launching SMB pricing tier; Opportunity: their complexity alienates SMB segment

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- âŒ Every competitor analyzed in detail
- âŒ Sales intelligence on competitive pricing
- âŒ Predictive competitive strategy
- âŒ Patent analysis or technology depth

### 4. Pattern Recognition & Insights âœ“

- [ ] **Data patterns identified**
  - Trends found in customer behavior data
  - Correlations identified (and causation assessed)
  - Anomalies or outliers explained
  - Patterns by segment, geography, time period, etc.

- [ ] **Root causes confirmed or revised**
  - SPLIT phase hypotheses validated against data
  - Root causes now grounded in evidence
  - Previously hidden drivers discovered
  - Root cause ranking by impact established

- [ ] **Key insights documented**
  - Major findings clearly stated
  - Supporting evidence provided
  - Implications for strategy captured
  - Questions answered from SPLIT phase

- [ ] **Systemic understanding developed**
  - How different factors interact understood
  - Feedback loops identified
  - Leverage points identified (where to apply pressure for change)
  - Ripple effects of different causes understood

**What This Looks Like:**
- **Patterns:** SMB customers churn after 18 months when they outgrow basic features; Enterprise customers more stable (3+ year retention)
- **Root Causes (Refined):**
  - Primary: Pricing misalignment for SMB segment (60% of churn)
  - Secondary: Feature gaps for growing SMB (30% of churn)
  - Tertiary: Competitive displacement (10% of churn)
- **Insight:** We're winning the customer acquisition battle but losing mid-market customers to scale-up competitor offering; we need pricing flexibility strategy
- **Leverage Point:** If we create SMB-specific pricing tier, we could retain 60% of churning customers; incremental revenue $3M+

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- âŒ Perfectly explained variance
- âŒ Complete causal proof
- âŒ Statistical models
- âŒ Predictive analytics

### 5. Data Quality & Rigor âœ“

- [ ] **Data sources credible and documented**
  - Sources identified for all major findings
  - Data quality assessed (clean, representative, timely)
  - Data limitations acknowledged
  - Proxy data or estimation noted where actual data unavailable

- [ ] **Analytical methodology transparent**
  - How data was analyzed explained
  - Tools/methods used documented
  - Analysis choices justified
  - Validation of findings (did we check our work?)

- [ ] **Assumptions explicit**
  - All major assumptions documented
  - Why assumptions were made (data gap, industry standard, expert judgment)
  - Risk if assumptions wrong assessed
  - Sensitivity to assumptions tested (where critical)

- [ ] **Confidence levels communicated**
  - Which findings are high-confidence (strong evidence)
  - Which are moderate-confidence (reasonable evidence, some gaps)
  - Which are exploratory/indicative (emerging pattern)
  - Caveats and limitations clearly stated

**What This Looks Like:**
- **Data Sources:** Customer churn data (CRM, validated against accounting); exit interviews (15 customers, purposive sample); competitive pricing (public websites, customer reports); market research (3rd-party report, 2 years old)
- **Methodology:** Churn analysis (SQL queries, triangulated across 3 sources); exit interviews (open-ended, coded for themes); competitive analysis (feature matrix, pricing sheets); market research (secondary)
- **Assumptions:** Exit interviews represent larger population (acknowledged bias toward more engaged customers); pricing data reflects public pricing only (actual deals likely different)
- **Confidence Levels:**
  - HIGH: SMB churn rates by cohort (internal data, clean)
  - MODERATE: Price sensitivity (exit interviews, market data, but small sample)
  - EXPLORATORY: Enterprise feature needs (few interviews, anecdotal evidence)

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- âŒ Primary research on all hypotheses
- âŒ Peer review of analysis
- âŒ Academic-level rigor
- âŒ Perfect data or methodology

### 6. Analysis Coherence & Completeness âœ“

- [ ] **All problem components addressed**
  - Each branch of problem tree analyzed
  - All questions from SPLIT phase answered (or explicitly noted as unanswered)
  - No major analytical gaps
  - Cross-component analysis done (how components interact)

- [ ] **Analysis answers strategic questions**
  - Root cause analysis addresses "why" questions
  - Market analysis answers "how big/important" questions
  - Competitive analysis answers "relative position" questions
  - Together, analysis informs strategic choices

- [ ] **Findings are actionable**
  - Analysis results lead to clear implications
  - Not just data for data's sake
  - Connects back to original problem and success criteria
  - Informs next phase (INSIGHT) thinking

**What This Looks Like:**
- **Original Problem:** Why are we losing customers?
- **Analysis Addresses:**
  - Root causes (pricing, features, competition)
  - Market dynamics (growing segment churning; stable segment loyal)
  - Competitive positioning (we're premium player losing to value players)
  - Financial impact (quantified revenue at risk)
- **Actionable Result:** Clear understanding of churn drivers; implication is need to segment strategy (premium for enterprise, value for SMB)

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- âŒ Analysis of every possible angle
- âŒ Answers to questions outside original scope
- âŒ Recommendations or solutions
- âŒ Complete clarity on all factors

## Enhancing Criteria (Improving Quality)

These items strengthen the phase but aren't strictly blocking:

- [ ] **Customer segment analysis completed**
  - Churn rates analyzed by segment (company size, industry, geography, tenure)

  - Segment-specific recommendations emerging
  - High-priority segments identified

- [ ] **Trend analysis conducted**
  - Is churn increasing, decreasing, stable?
  - Are competitive threats accelerating?
  - Are market dynamics shifting?
  - Leading indicators of future churn identified

- [ ] **Scenario planning initiated**
  - Best case scenario articulated (what if we fix all root causes?)
  - Worst case scenario articulated (what if trends accelerate?)
  - Most likely scenario described
  - Financial impact of each scenario modeled

- [ ] **Cross-functional validation completed**
  - Key findings reviewed with relevant business leaders
  - Disagreements surfaced and explored
  - Different perspectives integrated
  - Organizational buy-in beginning to form

- [ ] **Emerging opportunities identified**
  - Beyond just fixing problems, growth opportunities spotted
  - Competitive positioning improvements possible
  - Customer value creation possibilities seen
  - Innovation ideas emerging from data

## Quality Checks (Before Handoff)

Before confirming phase completion, ask yourself:

1. **Have we thoroughly tested the hypotheses?**
   - Did we find evidence for each major hypothesis?
   - Did we consider alternative explanations?
   - Would the findings hold up to scrutiny?

2. **Does the analysis answer the original questions?**
   - Can we explain why the problem exists?
   - Do we understand the business impact?
   - Can we identify root causes vs. symptoms?

3. **Is the analysis actionable?**
   - Do findings point toward possible solutions?
   - Can we understand what to fix?
   - Are priorities clear?

4. **Do the Analyst and Specialist both feel the analysis is complete?**
   - Do they have confidence in findings?
   - Are there nagging doubts or gaps?
   - Are they ready to hand off?

## Common Blockers & How to Resolve

| Blocker | Signal | Resolution |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Hypotheses incomplete** | Some untested | Prioritize critical ones; defer exploratory; document why |
| **Data unavailable** | "We don't have that" | Use proxies, conduct interviews, make assumptions explicit |
| **Analysis scattered** | No coherent narrative | Create summary document pulling findings together |
| **Findings unclear** | "Multiple possible interpretations" | Narrow down through additional analysis or expert judgment |
| **Confidence too low** | "We're not sure about this" | Accept lower confidence if data not available; note as limitation |
| **Over-analysis** | "We could keep analyzing forever" | Define minimal evidence needed; establish stop-point; move forward |

## Post-Completion Actions

Once this checklist passes:

1. **Create Analysis Summary Document**
   - Executive summary of findings
   - Key hypotheses and status
   - Root causes with supporting evidence
   - Competitive positioning
   - Financial impact

2. **Prepare Supporting Materials**
   - Detailed analysis by area
   - Methodology and data sources
   - Visualizations and charts
   - Raw data/transcripts archived

3. **Create Persona Handoff**
   - Use `persona-handoff-template.md`
   - Summarize key findings
   - Note root causes (confirmed)
   - Highlight what's clear vs. uncertain
   - Identify implications for next phase

4. **Coordinate with Communicator**
   - Share analysis summary
   - Prepare for synthesis phase
   - Identify key insights to emphasize
   - Note findings that need careful messaging

5. **Hand off to Strategic Communicator**
   - Share all analysis documents
   - Confirm Communicator ready to synthesize
   - Load INSIGHT phase files

6. **Archive ANALYSE Materials**
   - Save all analysis and data
   - Document methodologies
   - Reference for future phases
