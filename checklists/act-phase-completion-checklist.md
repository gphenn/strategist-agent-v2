# ACT Phase Completion Checklist

## Purpose

Validate that the ACT phase (Strategic Advisor + Strategic Innovator) has completed all essential work to transition from strategic planning to operational execution. This checklist ensures the strategic decision has been translated into a detailed, actionable, innovation-enriched implementation plan that guides organizational execution.

## What This Phase Accomplishes

The ACT phase operationalizes strategy. The Strategic Advisor and Innovator together ensure:
- Strategy is translated into executable plan
- Innovation opportunities are integrated
- Accountability and ownership are clear
- Resources are allocated and sequenced
- Success metrics are defined
- Organization is ready to execute

## Blocking Criteria (All Required)

### 1. Execution Plan Detailed & Sequenced ‚úì

- [ ] **Plan is broken into executable phases**
  - Quick wins/proof points (0-3 months)
  - Phase 1: Foundation building (0-6 months)
  - Phase 2: Scale/implementation (6-12 months)
  - Phase 3: Optimization/learning (12-24 months)
  - Clear milestones and gates between phases

- [ ] **Within each phase, activities are specific**
  - What specifically needs to happen? (Clear actions, not abstractions)
  - In what sequence? (Dependencies clear)
  - By when? (Dates or relative timing)
  - Who is responsible? (Owner assigned)
  - What's the success condition? (How do we know it's done?)

- [ ] **Critical path is identified**
  - What activities must happen first?
  - What activities are dependent on others?
  - Which activities are longest lead time?
  - What's the critical path to success?

**What This Looks Like:**
```
PHASE 1: FOUNDATION (Months 0-6)

Activity 1.1: Organizational Structure
- Create SMB Product Team (Product lead, 3 engineers, 1 designer)
- Create SMB GTM Team (Sales leader, 3 AEs, 1 marketing specialist)
- Establish governance (weekly steering committee)
- Timeline: Weeks 1-4 | Owner: VP Products | Success: Teams in place, charter defined

Activity 1.2: Product Requirements Definition
- Document simplified feature set for SMB
- Prioritize MVP features for launch
- Plan roadmap for years 1-3
- Timeline: Weeks 2-8 | Owner: SMB Product Lead | Success: PRD approved

Activity 1.3: Go-to-Market Strategy
- Define SMB positioning vs. enterprise
- Plan sales model (direct, partnerships, self-serve tiers)
- Create messaging framework
- Timeline: Weeks 3-8 | Owner: SMB Sales Lead | Success: GTM plan approved

Activity 1.4: Build Partnerships
- Identify potential channel partners
- Negotiate partnership terms
- Recruit partners for launch
- Timeline: Weeks 4-26 | Owner: SMB Sales Lead | Success: 3-5 partnerships signed

CRITICAL PATH:
- Hiring (slow; limit other timelines) ‚Üí Start Week 1
- Product requirements (blocks development) ‚Üí Complete by Week 8
- Partner identification (long sales cycles) ‚Üí Start Week 4

PHASE GATE: By end of Month 6, have teams in place, product plan set, partnerships identified
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Gantt chart level detail
- ‚ùå Every task specified
- ‚ùå Complete resource specifications
- ‚ùå Detailed budget breakdown

### 3. Resource Requirements & Allocation ‚úì

- [ ] **Resource needs identified by phase**
  - People requirements (headcount, roles, timeline)
  - Budget requirements (capital, operating expenses)
  - Technology/infrastructure needs
  - Partner/vendor requirements

- [ ] **Resource constraints acknowledged**
  - Total available budget vs. needed budget
  - Total available people vs. needed people
  - Are we within constraints or do we need to negotiate?
  - Where are the tightest constraints?

- [ ] **Contingency and flexibility planned**
  - If resources are tighter than planned, what gets deprioritized?
  - If we have more resources than expected, what gets accelerated?
  - What's the minimum viable resource set?
  - What's the optimal resource set?

**What This Looks Like:**
```
RESOURCE REQUIREMENTS:

- Phase 1 (Months 0-6): 8 new hires (product/gtm core team)
- Phase 2 (Months 6-12): 5 additional (expand teams, customer success)

- Phase 1: $1.5M (hiring, tools, partnerships)
- Phase 2: $2M (product development, sales ramp)
- Phase 3: $3M (scale, marketing, customer success)
- Total Year 1: $6.5M

- Available headcount budget: $10M across all initiatives (SMB is 13/50 total)
- ‚úì Headcount plan is feasible

### 4. Success Metrics & Tracking ‚úì

- [ ] **Success metrics defined for execution**
  - Business outcome metrics (revenue, profitability, retention)
  - Execution metrics (on-time delivery, quality, efficiency)
  - Leading indicators (pipeline, engagement, usage)
  - Frequency of tracking (daily, weekly, monthly)

- [ ] **Metrics connect to strategic objectives**
  - How do execution metrics serve strategic goal?
  - How will metrics inform course correction?
  - What's acceptable performance range?
  - What triggers intervention/escalation?

- [ ] **Targets are realistic and ambitious**
  - Targets are based on data and experience (not guesswork)
  - Targets are stretch (not easy to achieve)
  - Targets are achievable (not impossible)
  - Targets improve over time (Year 1 vs. Year 3)

**What This Looks Like:**
```
STRATEGIC OBJECTIVE: Build profitable SMB business to $15M ARR by Year 3

SUCCESS METRICS:

Product Development:
- MVP Launch: On time, quality <5 critical bugs at launch
- Feature Adoption: 60%+ users adopt simplified feature set within 3 months
- Customer Satisfaction: NPS >60 by end of Year 1

Sales & GTM:
- Sales Ramp: 3 AEs close $500K by Month 6, $300K/mo each by Month 12
- Partnership: 3-5 partners generating 20%+ of revenue by Year 2
- Customer Acquisition Cost: <12 month payback

Financial:
- Year 1 Revenue: $1M
- Year 2 Revenue: $8M
- Year 3 Revenue: $15M
- Gross Margin: >70% from day 1

Execution:
- Project Plans: On time delivery 80%+
- Quality: <3% customer escalations
- Team: Hiring on schedule; turnover <10%/year

TRACKING:
- Daily: Sales metrics (pipeline, activity)
- Weekly: Product metrics (development progress, customer feedback)
- Monthly: Financial review (revenue, expenses, cash)
- Quarterly: Strategic review (progress vs. plan, course correction)

INTERVENTION TRIGGERS:
- If revenue misses 20% vs. plan: Escalate to leadership; reassess GTM
- If customer churn >15%: Escalate to product; assess product-market fit
- If hiring delays >2 months: Escalate to CEO; identify barriers
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Detailed metric definitions
- ‚ùå Complete dashboard design
- ‚ùå All possible metrics (focus on key ones)
- ‚ùå Perfect forecasting

### 5. Innovation Integration ‚úì

- [ ] **Breakthrough opportunities integrated into plan**
  - Innovation ideas from INSIGHT/DECIDE surfaced
  - Which innovations are included in core plan?
  - Which innovations are in parallel/future tracks?
  - How are innovations resourced?

- [ ] **Innovation doesn't compromise core execution**
  - Core plan is solid (execution must happen)
  - Innovation adds to core (doesn't replace)
  - Innovation risks are mitigated
  - Resources for innovation are allocated separately

- [ ] **Pathways to breakthrough identified**
  - What does success look like for innovations?
  - How will we recognize if innovation is working?
  - What's the decision point to escalate or kill?
  - How do innovations feed into future phases?

**What This Looks Like:**

- Core plan: Monthly reviews, steering committee oversight

```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Detailed innovation project plans
- ‚ùå Complete innovation business cases

### 6. Risk Management & Contingencies ‚úì

- [ ] **Execution risks identified**
  - Resource risks (can we hire enough? retain people?)
  - Timeline risks (are estimates realistic?)
  - Market risks (will customers respond?)
  - Organizational risks (can we manage dual focus?)

- [ ] **Risk mitigation approaches defined**
  - For critical risks: What's our mitigation?
  - Is mitigation built into plan?
  - What's contingency if mitigation fails?
  - Who monitors this risk?

- [ ] **Contingency plans for critical scenarios**
  - If product development slips 3 months: What's plan B?
  - If SMB adoption slower than expected: How do we respond?
  - If competitor launches competitive offering: What's response?
  - What are go/no-go decision points?

**What This Looks Like:**
```
CRITICAL EXECUTION RISKS:

Risk 1: Can't hire/retain SMB team
- Probability: MEDIUM | Impact: HIGH (jeopardizes timeline)
- Mitigation: Hire early (month 1); offer competitive comp; clear vision
- Contingency: Outsource product to agency if hiring fails
- Go/No-Go: By Month 3, have SMB Product Lead; by Month 4, team core
- Monitor: Hiring pipeline monthly; retention metrics quarterly

Risk 2: Product development slips
- Probability: MEDIUM | Impact: MEDIUM (delays revenue, not fatal)
- Mitigation: Scope MVP ruthlessly; experienced team lead; frequent reviews
- Contingency: Extend Phase 1 by 3 months OR reduce feature scope
- Go/No-Go: By Month 6, must have MVP in customer testing
- Monitor: Development velocity weekly; customer feedback monthly

Risk 3: Sales adoption slower than expected
- Probability: HIGH | Impact: MEDIUM (revenue impact, fixable)
- Mitigation: Hire experienced SMB sales leader; clear incentives; early customer wins
- Contingency: Partner with integrators/resellers to fill gap
- Go/No-Go: By Month 6, AE must have first customer; by Month 12, $300K+ pipeline
- Monitor: Sales metrics weekly; pipeline health monthly

Risk 4: Dual focus distracts from enterprise
- Probability: MEDIUM | Impact: MEDIUM (enterprise churn risk)
- Mitigation: Separate teams; clear resource allocation; executive oversight
- Contingency: If enterprise loses customers, pause SMB scaling
- Go/No-Go: Monitor enterprise health monthly; threshold: >90% retention
- Monitor: Enterprise revenue/retention monthly; team satisfaction quarterly
```

**What This Does NOT Require:**
- ‚ùå Complete risk register
- ‚ùå Detailed mitigation plans
- ‚ùå Comprehensive contingency plans
- ‚ùå Risk-free execution (some risk inevitable)

## Quality Checks (Before Handoff)

Before confirming phase completion, ask yourself:

1. **Is this plan truly executable?**
   - Would a capable operations team understand what to do?
   - Are there any gaps or ambiguities?
   - Are resource needs realistic?
   - Is timeline achievable?

2. **Will we be able to track progress?**
   - Are success metrics clear?
   - Can we measure them?
   - Will we know if we're on track?
   - Will we catch problems early?

3. **Is the organization ready?**
   - Are teams aligned?
   - Do people understand their roles?
   - Will they have what they need?
   - Are risks and concerns addressed?

4. **Do we have a path forward if things go wrong?**
   - Are contingencies thoughtful?
   - Are decision points clear?
   - Will we know when to pivot?
   - Is there flexibility in the plan?

## Common Blockers & How to Resolve

| Blocker | Signal | Resolution |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Plan too ambitious** | "We can't do all this" | Ruthlessly prioritize; defer non-critical items |
| **Resources too tight** | "We don't have enough people/budget" | Accept trade-offs; defer initiatives; seek approval |
| **Metrics too many** | "We're tracking 20 KPIs" | Focus on 5-7 key metrics; defer others |
| **Stakeholder misalignment** | "Sales doesn't support this" | Address concerns; clarify rationale; secure buy-in |
| **Innovation too vague** | "We can't measure innovation success" | Define specific metrics; test incrementally |
| **Risk overwhelming** | "What if this all fails?" | Accept some risk; focus mitigation on critical risks |
| **Timeline unrealistic** | "Six months? We can't..." | Extend timeline OR reduce scope ruthlessly |
