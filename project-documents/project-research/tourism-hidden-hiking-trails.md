# Hidded Hiking Traild

Several **hidden hiking trails** on the Baie Verte Peninsula lead to **scenic viewpoints**, offering dramatic ocean vistas, iceberg sightings, and a sense of discovery off the beaten path. Here are some of the best options:

## Fleur de Lys Area

- **Ocean View Trail** (Fleur de Lys)
  - **Length:** 1.1 km (out-and-back)
  - **Difficulty:** Easy to moderate
  - **Highlights:** A short hike through woods leading to a viewpoint overlooking the ocean, ideal for spotting whales and icebergs in season. The trailhead is at the top of Lewis Lane in Fleur de Lys, with a small parking area. The route is family- and dog-friendly, and you’re likely to have the trail to yourself[2] [3].
- **Lookout Trail** (Fleur de Lys)
  - **Length:** 250 meters (one way)
  - **Highlights:** Begins at the Dorset Soapstone Museum and leads to a platform with panoramic views over the community and coastline[3].
- **Spotted Point Trail** (Fleur de Lys)
  - **Length:** 2 km (one way)
  - **Highlights:** A more challenging hike out to a rocky headland, offering dramatic cliffside views and, in season, iceberg spotting. Benches and picnic tables are available along the way, making it a pleasant outing for a rest or snack[3].

## Pacquet

- **Ocean View Lookout Trail**
  - **Length:** 1.6 miles (2.6 km)
  - **Highlights:** This trail in Pacquet is known for its beautiful outport scenery and ends at a great ocean viewpoint. It’s a local favorite for its accessibility and rewarding views[1].

## La Scie

- **La Scie Trails**
  - **Length:** 4 km (combined)
  - **Highlights:** La Scie, the farthest outport on the peninsula, offers five well-regarded trails, each leading to scenic viewpoints over the ocean and rugged coastline. These are community-maintained and less traveled, providing a more secluded hiking experience[1].

## Flatwater Pond Park (near Baie Verte)

- **Flatwater Pond Park Trails**
  - **Highlights:** Several trails in this area offer splendid views over the pond and surrounding forests. The park itself is a former logging camp, adding a touch of history to your hike[6].
