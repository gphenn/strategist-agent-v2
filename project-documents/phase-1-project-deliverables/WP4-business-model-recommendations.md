## Document Purpose

This document provides business model recommendations for each service opportunity identified in the Business Opportunity Analysis. Recommendations will be refined with detailed service requirements and contract terms from Firefly Metals engagement.

**Objective:** Provide actionable business model recommendations to guide business development and service provider readiness.

**Target:** 80-85% of mining service contracts captured locally (from strategic context brief)

## Business Model Framework

### Business Model Components

**For Each Service Opportunity:**

1. **Service Delivery Model:**
   - Service delivery approach
   - Service location (on-site, off-site, hybrid)
   - Service frequency and schedule

2. **Revenue Model:**
   - Pricing structure
   - Payment terms
   - Revenue streams

3. **Operations Model:**
   - Operational approach
   - Staffing model
   - Quality assurance

4. **Partnership Model:**
   - Partnership structure (if applicable)
   - Joint venture options
   - Consortium options

## Business Model Recommendations by Service Category

### 1. Camp Cafeteria Services Business Model

#### Recommended Model: Full-Service On-Site Camp Cafeteria

**Service Delivery Model:**
- **Location:** On-site at camp facility
- **Service Type:** Full-service cafeteria
- **Service Frequency:** Daily (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks)
- **Operating Hours:** [To be determined from Firefly engagement]

**Revenue Model Options:**

**Option A: Per-Meal Pricing**
- **Structure:** Charge per meal served
- **Advantages:** Direct cost recovery, flexible pricing
- **Disadvantages:** Revenue variability, billing complexity
- **Recommended:** No (prefer stability)

**Option B: Per-Worker Contract (Fixed Monthly Fee)**
- **Structure:** Fixed monthly fee per worker
- **Advantages:** Predictable revenue, simplified billing
- **Disadvantages:** Fixed pricing regardless of usage
- **Recommended:** Yes (preferred for stability)

**Option C: Hybrid Model (Base Contract + Per-Meal)**
- **Structure:** Base monthly fee + per-meal charges
- **Advantages:** Base revenue stability + usage-based revenue
- **Disadvantages:** More complex billing
- **Recommended:** Yes (alternative option)

**Recommended Revenue Model:** Option B or C

**Operations Model:**
- **Staffing:** Kitchen staff, serving staff, management
- **Quality Assurance:** Food service certification, health standards, quality monitoring
- **Supply Chain:** Food suppliers, inventory management
- **Facilities:** Kitchen facilities, dining facilities, storage

**Partnership Model:**
- **Option 1:** Single local business
- **Option 2:** Joint venture (local + regional partner)
- **Option 3:** Consortium of local businesses
- **Recommended:** Option 1 or 2 (depending on capacity)

**Status:** ⚠️ Requires Firefly engagement for contract terms

## Partnership Model Recommendations

### Partnership Model Options

#### Option 1: Single Local Business

**Description:** Single local business provides service independently

**Advantages:**
- Simple structure
- Direct control
- Full revenue retention
- Local ownership

**Disadvantages:**
- Limited capacity
- Higher risk
- Limited resources

**Application:** Smaller service contracts, existing business expansion

---

#### Option 2: Joint Venture

**Description:** Partnership between local business and regional/national partner

**Advantages:**
- Shared investment
- Shared risk
- Combined expertise
- Larger capacity

**Disadvantages:**
- Revenue sharing
- Complexity
- Coordination required

**Application:** Large service contracts, capacity building support

---

#### Option 3: Consortium

**Description:** Multiple local businesses form consortium for large contracts

**Advantages:**
- Combined capacity
- Shared resources
- Risk sharing
- Competitive positioning

**Disadvantages:**
- Coordination complexity
- Revenue sharing
- Management complexity

**Application:** Very large service contracts, complex service requirements
