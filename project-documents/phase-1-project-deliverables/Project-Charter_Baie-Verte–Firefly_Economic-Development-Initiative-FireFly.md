# Project Charter: Baie Verte–Firefly Economic Development Initiative

## 1\. Project Title

Baie Verte–Firefly Economic Development Initiative

## 2\. Project Purpose & Vision

To position Firefly Metals as a catalyst for sustainable economic growth and community prosperity in Baie Verte, Newfoundland, by integrating mining operations with local workforce, business, and infrastructure development.

## 3\. Objectives

* Maximize local employment and workforce readiness to support Firefly’s operational needs.  
* Expand local business and supplier participation in Firefly’s supply chain.  
* Upgrade community infrastructure to ensure operational efficiency and support workforce attraction/retention.  
* Secure and align government funding and incentives to de-risk and accelerate project execution.  
* Demonstrate leadership in ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) and social license through proactive community and Indigenous engagement.

## 4\. Scope

In Scope:

* Workforce training and upskilling programs aligned with Firefly’s talent requirements.  
* Supplier readiness and business development initiatives to increase local procurement.  
* Infrastructure planning and project execution (housing, transport, utilities) supporting Firefly’s operations.  
* Grant writing and funding application management for Firefly and community-aligned projects.  
* Community and Indigenous engagement activities to strengthen Firefly’s social license.

Out of Scope:

* Direct management of non-Firefly community projects.  
* Activities outside the Baie Verte region.

## 5\. Key Stakeholders

* Project Sponsor: Firefly Metals Executive Team  
* Strategic Partners: Baie Verte Town Council, ACOA, CBDC, Provincial Government  
* Community Partners: Local businesses, Indigenous communities, residents, educational institutions

## 6\. Governance Structure

* Task Force: Multi-stakeholder steering committee (Firefly, Town, ACOA/CBDC, Indigenous, business reps)  
* Project Manager: \[Name/To be assigned, Firefly or joint\]  
* Working Groups: Workforce, Supplier, Infrastructure, Engagement, Funding

## 7\. Major Deliverables

* Workforce and supplier audit reports tailored to Firefly’s operational needs  
* Training and supplier development programs with Firefly input  
* Infrastructure project proposals and funding applications supporting Firefly’s growth  
* Community engagement plan and public forum schedule  
* Quarterly progress and KPI reports for Firefly leadership

## 8\. Timeline

* Mobilization: 0–3 months  
* Program Design & Funding: 3–6 months  
* Execution: 6–18 months  
* Monitoring & Adaptation: Ongoing

## 9\. Success Criteria / KPIs

* % increase in local hiring and training completions for Firefly roles  
* $ value of local procurement and new business creation in Firefly’s supply chain  
* Amount of funding secured for Firefly/community-aligned projects  
* Infrastructure projects completed on time/budget supporting Firefly’s operations  
* Community and Indigenous satisfaction metrics (social license indicators)

## 10\. Risks & Mitigation

* Workforce shortages: Early training, targeted recruitment, partnership with local institutions  
* Supplier gaps: Supplier readiness program, business support, Firefly procurement engagement  
* Funding delays: Dedicated grant-writing team, early applications, Firefly advocacy  
* Community concerns: Transparent engagement, regular forums, ESG leadership
