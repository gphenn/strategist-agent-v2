# Project Charter: Baie Verte–Firefly Economic Development Initiative

## 1\. Project Title

Baie Verte–Firefly Economic Development Initiative

## 2\. Project Purpose & Vision

To leverage the Firefly Metals mining project as a catalyst for sustainable economic growth, workforce development, and community prosperity in Baie Verte, Newfoundland.

## 3\. Objectives

* Maximize local employment and workforce readiness.  
* Expand local business and supplier participation in mining and related sectors.  
* Upgrade community infrastructure to support growth.  
* Secure and align government funding and incentives.  
* Ensure environmental stewardship and strong community engagement.

## 4\. Scope

In Scope:

* Workforce training and upskilling programs.  
* Supplier readiness and business development initiatives.  
* Infrastructure planning and project execution (housing, transport, utilities).  
* Grant writing and funding application management.  
* Community and Indigenous engagement activities.

Out of Scope:

* Direct management of Firefly Metals’ mining operations.  
* Activities outside the Baie Verte region.

## 5\. Key Stakeholders

* Project Sponsor: Baie Verte Town Council  
* Strategic Partner: Firefly Metals  
* Funding Partners: ACOA, CBDC, Provincial Government  
* Community Partners: Local businesses, Indigenous communities, residents, educational institutions

## 6\. Governance Structure

* Task Force: Multi-stakeholder steering committee (Town, Firefly, ACOA/CBDC, Indigenous, business reps)  
* Project Manager: \[Name/To be assigned\]  
* Working Groups: Workforce, Supplier, Infrastructure, Engagement, Funding

## 7\. Major Deliverables

* Workforce and supplier audit reports  
* Training and supplier development programs  
* Infrastructure project proposals and funding applications  
* Community engagement plan and public forum schedule  
* Quarterly progress and KPI reports

## 8\. Timeline

* Mobilization: 0–3 months  
* Program Design & Funding: 3–6 months  
* Execution: 6–18 months  
* Monitoring & Adaptation: Ongoing

## 9\. Success Criteria / KPIs

* % increase in local hiring and training completions  
* $ value of local procurement and new business creation  
* Amount of funding secured  
* Infrastructure projects completed on time/budget  
* Community and Indigenous satisfaction metrics

## 10\. Risks & Mitigation

* Workforce shortages: Early training, targeted recruitment  
* Supplier gaps: Supplier readiness program, business support  
* Funding delays: Dedicated grant-writing team, early applications  
* Community concerns: Transparent engagement, regular forums
