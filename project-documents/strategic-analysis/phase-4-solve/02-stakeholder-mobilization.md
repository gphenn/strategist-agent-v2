# Stakeholder Mobilization & Coordination

**Strategic Specialist (Morgan) - Stakeholder Activation & Coordination**

## Executive Summary

This document provides the comprehensive stakeholder mobilization and coordination framework for executing the Baie Verte mining expansion economic development strategy, focusing on activating all key stakeholders and establishing effective coordination mechanisms.

## Stakeholder Mobilization Strategy

### Stakeholder Activation Framework

#### Primary Stakeholder Groups

1. **Municipal Government (Town of Baie Verte)**
   - **Role**: Central coordination and leadership
   - **Activation Priority**: Immediate (Week 1)
   - **Key Representatives**: Mayor, CAO, Economic Development Officer, Council Members
   - **Commitment Level**: High (Primary responsibility)

2. **Firefly Metals**
   - **Role**: Primary customer and partner
   - **Activation Priority**: Immediate (Week 1-2)
   - **Key Representatives**: President & CEO, VPs, Operations Manager
   - **Commitment Level**: High (Direct economic interest)

3. **Provincial Agencies**
   - **Role**: Funding and support providers
   - **Activation Priority**: High (Week 2-4)
   - **Key Representatives**: IET, MA, TI, JG, RED representatives
   - **Commitment Level**: Medium-High (Programmatic support)

4. **Federal Agencies**
   - **Role**: Additional funding and support
   - **Activation Priority**: Medium (Week 3-6)
   - **Key Representatives**: ACOA, Natural Resources Canada representatives
   - **Commitment Level**: Medium (Programmatic support)

5. **Indigenous Groups (Qalipu First Nation)**
   - **Role**: Partnership and consultation
   - **Activation Priority**: High (Week 2-4)
   - **Key Representatives**: Chief, Council, Economic Development Officer
   - **Commitment Level**: Medium (Partnership interest)

6. **Regional Communities**
   - **Role**: Collaboration and partnership
   - **Activation Priority**: Medium (Week 4-8)
   - **Key Representatives**: Municipal leaders, economic development officers
   - **Commitment Level**: Medium (Collaboration interest)

## Coordination Framework

### Baie Verte Mining Services Coordination Committee

#### Committee Structure

**Steering Committee (High-Level Oversight)**

- **Chair**: Municipal Mayor
- **Members**: Municipal CAO, Firefly Metals President, Provincial Agency Representatives, Indigenous Representative
- **Frequency**: Monthly meetings
- **Purpose**: Strategic oversight, major decision-making, stakeholder coordination

**Executive Committee (Operational Management)**

- **Chair**: Municipal CAO
- **Members**: Municipal Economic Development Officer, Firefly Operations Manager, Provincial Program Managers
- **Frequency**: Bi-weekly meetings
- **Purpose**: Operational coordination, progress monitoring, issue resolution

**Working Groups (Specialized Focus)**

- **Infrastructure Working Group**: Infrastructure planning and implementation
- **Workforce Working Group**: Workforce development and training
- **Business Development Working Group**: Business support and service capture
- **Stakeholder Relations Working Group**: Stakeholder engagement and communication

#### Committee Charter

**Mission**: Coordinate stakeholder efforts to maximize local economic benefits from the mining expansion

**Objectives**:

1. Establish and maintain stakeholder coordination mechanisms
2. Monitor progress and performance of all strategic initiatives
3. Facilitate communication and collaboration among stakeholders
4. Identify and resolve issues and challenges
5. Ensure alignment with strategic objectives and success metrics

**Roles and Responsibilities**:

- **Steering Committee**: Strategic oversight, major decisions, stakeholder coordination
- **Executive Committee**: Operational management, progress monitoring, issue resolution
- **Working Groups**: Specialized planning and implementation
- **Secretariat**: Administrative support, meeting coordination, documentation

**Decision-Making Framework**:

- **Strategic Decisions**: Steering committee approval required
- **Operational Decisions**: Executive committee authority
- **Technical Decisions**: Working group authority with executive committee oversight
- **Stakeholder Decisions**: Advisory input with executive committee coordination

### Stakeholder Engagement Strategies

#### Engagement Levels

**Level 1: Inform**

- **Stakeholders**: General public, community members
- **Methods**: Public meetings, newsletters, website updates
- **Frequency**: Monthly
- **Purpose**: Keep informed of progress and developments

**Level 2: Consult**

- **Stakeholders**: Regional communities, business community
- **Methods**: Surveys, focus groups, stakeholder meetings
- **Frequency**: Quarterly
- **Purpose**: Gather input and feedback on initiatives

**Level 3: Involve**

- **Stakeholders**: Provincial agencies, federal agencies
- **Methods**: Working groups, advisory committees, regular meetings
- **Frequency**: Monthly
- **Purpose**: Active involvement in planning and implementation

**Level 4: Collaborate**

- **Stakeholders**: Firefly Metals, Indigenous groups
- **Methods**: Partnership agreements, joint planning, shared decision-making
- **Frequency**: Bi-weekly
- **Purpose**: Collaborative planning and implementation

**Level 5: Empower**

- **Stakeholders**: Municipal government, key stakeholders
- **Methods**: Delegated authority, decision-making power, resource allocation
- **Frequency**: Continuous
- **Purpose**: Empowered decision-making and implementation
