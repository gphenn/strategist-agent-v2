# Strategic Framework: Baie Verte Mining Expansion Economic Development

## Strategic Specialist (Morgan) - Comprehensive Strategic Integration

## Executive Summary

This strategic framework integrates all components from the Split step into a unified approach for maximizing local economic benefits from Firefly Metals' mining expansion in Baie Verte, Newfoundland.

## Vision Statement

"Baie Verte as the premier mining services hub of Newfoundland, capturing 80-85% of mining service contracts locally while building sustainable economic capacity for long-term community prosperity."

## Mission Statement

"To coordinate stakeholder efforts, develop local business capacity, and optimize infrastructure to maximize economic capture from the mining expansion while ensuring sustainable community development."

## Strategic Pillars

### Pillar 1: Stakeholder Coordination & Alignment

**Objective**: Establish formal coordination mechanisms to maximize stakeholder collaboration and local economic capture.

**Strategic Elements**:

- **Municipal Leadership**: Central coordination role for all stakeholder engagement
- **Firefly Partnership**: Formal procurement and local hiring commitments
- **Provincial Integration**: Leverage all available funding and support programs
- **Indigenous Collaboration**: Meaningful partnership with Qalipu First Nation
- **Regional Cooperation**: Strategic partnerships with neighboring communities

**Key Success Indicators**:

- Formal coordination agreements established
- Stakeholder engagement protocols implemented
- Regular coordination meetings and reporting
- Clear communication channels and decision-making processes

### Pillar 2: Infrastructure Readiness & Investment

**Objective**: Complete critical infrastructure upgrades to enable optimal service delivery and economic development.

**Strategic Elements**:

- **Highway Upgrades**: Accommodate increased traffic and transportation needs
- **Cell Coverage**: Ensure communication and safety for all operations
- **Healthcare Capacity**: Expand services to meet workforce needs
- **Power & Water**: Increase capacity for camp and service operations
- **Municipal Services**: Enhance capacity for expanded operations

**Key Success Indicators**:

- Infrastructure projects completed by Q2 2026
- All critical infrastructure capacity requirements met
- Funding secured for all infrastructure projects
- Regulatory approvals obtained for all projects

### Pillar 3: Workforce Development & Capacity Building

**Objective**: Develop comprehensive workforce capacity to meet service delivery requirements and create employment opportunities.

**Strategic Elements**:

- **Training Programs**: Aligned with service requirements and industry standards
- **Certification Support**: Safety and technical certification programs
- **Recruitment Strategy**: Local and regional workforce attraction
- **Retention Programs**: Competitive compensation and career development
- **Indigenous Inclusion**: Meaningful participation and training opportunities

**Key Success Indicators**:

- Workforce development program initiated by Q3 2025
- Training capacity established for all service categories
- Recruitment targets met for all service areas
- Retention rates maintained at competitive levels

### Pillar 4: Business Development & Service Capture

**Objective**: Enable local businesses to scale and compete effectively for mining service contracts.

**Strategic Elements**:

- **Financial Support**: Access to capital and funding for business expansion
- **Technical Assistance**: Capacity building and quality improvement support
- **Procurement Navigation**: Support for competitive bidding processes
- **Partnership Facilitation**: Strategic partnerships and joint ventures
- **Market Development**: Expansion beyond mining services

**Key Success Indicators**:

- 80-85% of mining service contracts captured locally
- Business development support program implemented
- New businesses created or expanded
- Service quality and reliability maintained
