## Leverage Point Analysis

### High-Leverage Components
1. **Municipal Government Coordination**
   - **Impact**: Affects all stakeholder relationships
   - **Leverage**: Central coordination role
   - **Dependencies**: Staff capacity, political will, funding

2. **Provincial Agency Engagement**
   - **Impact**: Funding, regulatory support, technical assistance
   - **Leverage**: Multiple support programs and resources
   - **Dependencies**: Clear project proposals, alignment with provincial priorities

3. **Firefly Metals Partnership**
   - **Impact**: Direct service contracts, procurement processes
   - **Leverage**: Primary customer and decision-maker
   - **Dependencies**: Quality, reliability, competitive pricing

4. **Infrastructure Investment**
   - **Impact**: Enables all service delivery
   - **Leverage**: Foundation for economic development
   - **Dependencies**: Funding, regulatory approval, construction capacity

### Bottleneck Components
1. **Funding Availability**
   - **Risk**: Delays infrastructure and business development
   - **Mitigation**: Diversified funding sources, staged approach
   - **Dependencies**: Government budgets, private investment

2. **Workforce Capacity**
   - **Risk**: Limits service delivery and quality
   - **Mitigation**: Early training programs, recruitment strategies
   - **Dependencies**: Training capacity, competitive compensation

3. **Regulatory Approval**
   - **Risk**: Delays infrastructure and business development
   - **Mitigation**: Early engagement, compliance planning
   - **Dependencies**: Government processes, environmental assessment

4. **Regional Competition**
   - **Risk**: Reduces service capture and economic impact
   - **Mitigation**: Strategic positioning, quality advantages
   - **Dependencies**: Market analysis, competitive strategies

## Feedback Loop Analysis

### Positive Feedback Loops
1. **Service Quality → Contract Retention → Economic Impact → Investment → Service Quality**
   - Early success builds reputation and capacity
   - Economic benefits enable further investment
   - Improved services increase competitive advantage

2. **Workforce Development → Service Delivery → Economic Benefits → Community Support → Workforce Development**
   - Training creates employment opportunities
   - Economic benefits increase community support
   - Support enables further workforce development

3. **Infrastructure Investment → Service Capacity → Economic Impact → Tax Revenue → Infrastructure Investment**
   - Infrastructure enables service delivery
   - Economic impact increases municipal revenue
   - Revenue enables further infrastructure investment

### Negative Feedback Loops
1. **Timeline Pressure → Quality Compromise → Performance Issues → Contract Loss → Timeline Pressure**
   - Aggressive timelines may compromise quality
   - Quality issues affect contract retention
   - Contract loss increases timeline pressure

2. **Funding Constraints → Limited Investment → Service Capacity → Economic Impact → Funding Constraints**
   - Limited funding restricts investment
   - Limited investment reduces service capacity
   - Reduced capacity limits economic impact
   - Limited impact reduces funding availability

## Risk Propagation Analysis

### Primary Risk Propagation Paths
1. **Infrastructure Delay → Service Delivery Delay → Contract Loss → Economic Impact Reduction**
   - Mitigation: Parallel development tracks, contingency planning

2. **Workforce Shortage → Quality Issues → Performance Problems → Reputation Damage**
   - Mitigation: Early workforce development, retention strategies

3. **Funding Uncertainty → Investment Delay → Capacity Limitations → Service Constraints**
   - Mitigation: Diversified funding sources, staged approach

4. **Regulatory Delay → Timeline Extension → Cost Increases → Viability Concerns**
   - Mitigation: Early regulatory engagement, compliance planning

### Risk Isolation Strategies
1. **Modular Development**: Independent development of different service areas
2. **Staged Investment**: Incremental funding and development approach
3. **Diversified Partnerships**: Multiple stakeholder and funding sources
4. **Contingency Planning**: Alternative approaches for critical components
